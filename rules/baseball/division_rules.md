# RAMMS Baseball Rules: Division-Specific Rules

Divisions in ascending order of age/skill: **Farm League** (youngest) → **Ranch League** → **American League** → **National League** (oldest). Farm and Ranch are coach-pitch; American and National are kid-pitch.

**1. Eligibility / Entry Point:**
- **Farm:** Pre-school children may play with Farm Commissioner and coach permission. Entry-level, tee-rescue division.
- **Ranch:** Kindergarten children may play with Ranch Commissioner and coach permission.
- **American & National:** Players may play up or down between the Ranch, American, and National Leagues; playing down requires the lower Commissioner's approval, while playing up is decided by the upper league's coaches and Commissioner at the draft.

**2. Field Dimensions:**
- **Farm:** Base path 45 ft; pitching distance 20–35 ft (coach's discretion).
- **Ranch:** Base path 50 ft; pitching mound 35 ft.
- **American:** Bases 60 ft; pitching mound 46 ft.
- **National:** Bases 70 ft; pitching mound 50 ft.

**3. Pitching Type:**
- **Farm:** Coach-pitch; up to 5 pitches per at bat (no balls/strikes counted), then a batting "tee rescue" if the batter cannot put the ball in play. All walks prohibited.
- **Ranch:** Coach-pitch; maximum 7 pitches or 3 strikes per at bat; extra pitches only when fouling the 7th or later; no advancing to first on a dropped third strike.
- **American:** Kid-pitch. No breaking balls; one mound visit per pitcher per inning; intentional walks prohibited.
- **National:** Kid-pitch. No breaking balls; one mound visit per pitcher per inning; intentional walks prohibited; **balks may be called** at the umpire's discretion.

**4. Pitch Counts & Rest (kid-pitch divisions only):**
- **American:** Tier 1 = 1–45 pitches (1 day rest); Tier 2 = 46–65 max (3 days rest); maximum 100 pitches per 7 consecutive days.
- **National:** Tier 1 = 1–55 pitches (1 day rest); Tier 2 = 56–75 max (3 days rest); maximum 110 pitches per 7 consecutive days.
- A player who throws over the Tier 1 limit may not catch in the same game. Pitch counts do not reset for, and are still counted during, the playoffs.

**5. Defensive Alignment:**
- **Farm:** Field the normal infield (1B, 2B, SS, 3B, P) except catcher; remaining players in the outfield behind the infield dirt.
- **Ranch & American:** Field ten (10) players with four (4) outfielders at the same general depth, at least 20 ft from the infield baseline.
- **National:** Field nine (9) players (standard alignment).

**6. Base Running — Leads & Stealing:**
- **Farm & Ranch:** No stealing or leading off; runner may not leave until the ball is hit. (Ranch additionally bars a runner on third from scoring on a passed ball or wild pitch.)
- **American:** No leads; runner may not leave until the pitch crosses home plate, then may advance to any base. First violation warns the team; subsequent violations call the runner out.
- **National:** Leads are allowed; a runner may advance to any base, including home, before the pitch crosses the plate.

**7. Infield Fly Rule:**
- **Farm, Ranch, American:** Not in effect.
- **National:** In effect.

**8. Dropped Third Strike:**
- **Farm:** N/A (coach-pitch, 5 pitches, no strikes counted).
- **Ranch:** No advancing to first on a dropped third strike (coach-pitch).
- **American:** The batter may not advance on a catcher's dropped third strike.
- **National:** A base runner may not advance safely to first on a catcher's dropped third strike.

**9. Bunting:**
- **Farm & Ranch:** Bunting is **not** allowed.
- **American & National:** Bunting is allowed.

**10. Game Length & Time Limit:**
- **Farm:** 4 innings; new inning not started after 1 hour; inning ends on the 3rd out **or the 8th batter**.
- **Ranch, American, National:** 6 innings; new inning not started after 1½ hours; inning ends on the 3rd out.

**11. Run Limit:**
- **Farm:** Four-run rule (inning ends at 3 outs, 4 runs, or return to the lead-off batter).
- **Ranch, American, National:** Six-run rule (cap of 6 runs per inning, or enough to take a 6-run lead if trailing).

**12. Playoff Tie / Minimum Innings:**
- **Farm:** At least 4 innings in the playoffs; regular-season ties stand; playoff ties use a runner on 2nd base and two outs to start each extra half-inning until a winner is decided.
- **Ranch, American, National:** At least 6 innings in the playoffs.

**13. Minimum Players to Avoid Forfeit:**
- **Farm & Ranch:** Fewer than 8 regular players → game rescheduled, or forfeited if it cannot be rescheduled.
- **American:** Fewer than 7 regular players → forfeit; minimum of 8 to field a team; roster may be filled (up to 10) with Ranch League substitutes.
- **National:** Fewer than 6 regular players → forfeit; minimum of 8 to field a team; roster may be filled with American League substitutes.

**14. Overthrow Out of Play (special award):**
- **Ranch:** Runners advance the base they were running to **plus one** additional base (two bases total).
- **Farm:** Runners advance one additional base.
- **American & National:** Awarded per the Official Rules of Baseball.
