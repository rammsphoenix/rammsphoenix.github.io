# RAMMS Baseball Rules: Common to All Divisions

These rules apply across all four divisions (Farm, Ranch, American, National), with only minor wording differences. Division-specific values are listed separately in `division_rules.md`.

**1. Purpose:** The intent of the program is to provide clean, wholesome recreation for a maximum number of youngsters, helping young people learn to win as well as lose while preparing them for further baseball opportunities.

**2. Governing Rules:** Games are played according to the Official Rules of Baseball, except as modified by the division rules. The RAMMS Code of Conduct is incorporated.

**3. Participation:** All players have the opportunity to play at least three innings of every game in the field, and all players must play at least every other inning on defense, disciplinary action notwithstanding. (Farm references the first three of four innings; the others reference at least three of the first five innings.)

**4. Batting Order:** Batting is on a bat-a-round basis. All rostered players who appear and are not injured are listed in the batting order and bat in turn.

**5. Late Arrivals:** A late-arriving player is added to the bottom of the batting order.

**6. Injured Batters:** An injured player's place in the order is skipped without penalty; the player may re-enter and must resume his prior place in the order.

**7. Bats:** All wood bats are permitted. Non-wood bats may have a barrel size up to 2 5/8" and must be stamped under current USA Bat (or BBCOR) standards. (Farm restricts to USA Bat only and excludes USSSA-stamped bats.)

**8. Offensive Interference:** If an offensive player deliberately runs into a defensive player to force a dropped ball, the offensive player is called out and the play is dead. (Penalty for other runners differs slightly by division — see division rules.)

**9. Obstruction:** A defensive player who blocks a base or impedes a runner without possession of the ball (or while not receiving it) gives the runner the base he was proceeding to.

**10. Head-First Slides:** A head-first slide results in an out, except when returning to a base already reached safely.

**11. Slide to Avoid Contact:** When a defensive player is receiving or holding the ball to make a tag, all runners must slide to avoid contact or be called out.

**12. No Infield Fly (most divisions):** The infield fly rule is not in effect in Farm, Ranch, and American. (National is the exception — infield fly is in effect.)

**13. Substitutions Intent:** It is the intent of RAMMS to avoid loss of play caused by lack of players. Substitute RAMMS players play in the outfield and bat last, can be displaced by an arriving roster player, and are subject to Commissioner approval.

**14. Game Time Limit Framework:** A new inning will not start once the division's time limit has expired; all innings started before the limit are completed (no drop-dead time). If a game ends due to no lights, the score reverts to the last completed inning (no makeup innings).

**15. Lights-Out Curfew:** All play must stop no later than 8:45 p.m. (Mon–Thu) and 9:45 p.m. (Fri–Sat).

**16. Official Game Clock:** The umpire maintains an official game clock; the home scorekeeper records the time of the first pitch. Intentional delays or speed-ups to circumvent the time limit may result in forfeiture at the umpire's discretion.

**17. Home Team Duties:** The Home Team maintains the official scorebook (and, in kid-pitch divisions, official pitch counts), occupies the third base dugout, and reports the final score within forty-eight (48) hours.

**18. Warm-ups:** The Home Team may take the field 30 minutes before game time and the Visiting Team 15 minutes before, each for a 15-minute infield/outfield warm-up. No live batting practice on the field.

**19. Game Balls:** Each coach keeps a copy of the rules at every game, and each team contributes one new ball and one back-up ball as game balls.

**20. Playoffs:** Playoffs are conducted pursuant to RAMMS Board Policy.

**21. Eligibility — One League:** A child shall only play in one league.

**22. Tryout Requirement:** A player wanting to play up must attend tryouts for BOTH the lower and upper leagues. Commissioners may reassign players who miss a tryout and may grant special exemptions.

**23. No Stealing (coach-pitch divisions):** Stealing and leading off are prohibited in Farm and Ranch; runners may not leave the base until the ball is hit.
