RAMMS Baseball Rules

National League

Applies to all divisions
Specific to this division
1. Field & Equipment
1.1 Base Paths & Pitching Distance Division Rule

Bases: 70 ft.  ·  Pitching mound: 50 ft.

1.2 Bats

All wood bats are permitted. Non-wood bats are permitted and may have a barrel size up to two and five-eighth inches (2 5/8″) provided any non-wood bats are stamped under current USA Bat or BBCOR bat standards.

1.3 Governing Rules Reference

Games are played according to the Official Rules of Baseball, except as modified below. The RAMMS Code of Conduct is incorporated herein.

2. Before the Game
2.1 Home Team & Dugout

The Home Team will maintain the official scorebook and official pitch counts, and will occupy the third base dugout.

2.2 Warm-ups

The Home Team may take the field thirty (30) minutes before game time for a fifteen (15) minute infield and outfield warm-up. The Visiting Team may take the field fifteen (15) minutes before game time for a fifteen (15) minute warm-up. No live batting practice is allowed on the field.

2.3 Game Balls & Score Reporting

Each coach must maintain a copy of the rules at every game. Each team contributes one (1) new ball and one back-up ball to the umpire as the game balls. The Home Team enters the final score in their scorebook and reports it to the League Commissioner within forty-eight (48) hours.

3. Game Format
3.1 Innings

A game consists of six (6) innings and no extra innings are played (except for playoffs).

3.2 Time Limit

A new inning will not start if one and one-half (1½) hours has expired. An inning starts immediately after the third out of the preceding inning.

3.3 Completing Innings

All innings started prior to the time limit will be completed. There is no drop-dead time. If the game is concluded due to no lights, the score reverts to the last completed inning (no makeup innings).

3.4 Official Game Clock

The umpire maintains an official game clock and the home team’s official scorekeeper should record the time of the first pitch in the official scorebook. Any intentional “delays” or “speed-ups” to circumvent the time limit may result in forfeiture at the umpire’s discretion.

3.5 Lights-Out Curfew

All play must stop no later than 8:45 p.m. (M–TR) and 9:45 p.m. (Fri–Sat). In this event the score reverts to the last completed inning (no makeup innings).

4. Participation & Playing Time
4.1 Defensive Participation

It is the policy of RAMMS that all players have the opportunity to play at least three of the first five innings of every game in the field. All players must play at least every other inning in the field on defense, disciplinary action notwithstanding.

5. Batting
5.1 Batting Order

Batting is on a bat-a-round basis. All players on the roster who appear for the game and who are not injured are listed in the batting order and given the opportunity to bat in turn.

5.2 Late Arrivals

If a player arrives late, their name is added to the bottom of the batting order.

5.3 Injured Batters

If a player is injured and cannot continue, their position in the batting order is skipped without penalty to the offensive team. The player may re-enter the game but must resume the place in the batting order he occupied before the injury.

5.4 Bunting Division Rule

Players are allowed to bunt.

6. Fielding
6.1 Defensive Alignment Division Rule

Teams field nine (9) players defensively.

7. Pitching
7.1 Player Pitching Division Rule

Players pitch in this league (kid-pitch).

7.2 Pitch Counts & Rest Division Rule

Tier one: a player who throws 1–55 game pitches requires 1 calendar day of rest. (For example, a pitcher who throws his Tier-one limit on Monday cannot pitch again until Wednesday – he must rest Tuesday. One calendar day does not equal 24 hours from his last pitch of his last game to the first pitch of his next game.) If a player catches and then pitches in the same game, he may not throw more than 55 pitches; a pitcher who throws 55 or less may catch in the same game, but a pitcher who throws more than 55 may not catch in the same game.

Tier two: a player who throws 56–75 (maximum) pitches requires 3 calendar days of rest. (For example, a pitcher who throws 75 pitches on Monday cannot pitch again until Friday – he must rest Tuesday, Wednesday, and Thursday.) A player who falls into this tier may not catch in the same game.

Maximum: a player may not throw more than 110 pitches in seven (7) consecutive calendar days. Pitch counts do not reset prior to the playoffs and are still counted during the playoffs.

Each player’s pitch count must be recorded in the official scorebook. Pitch counts are also recorded with the commissioner at the time the score is sent in.

7.3 No Breaking Balls Division Rule

No breaking balls may be thrown in this league. At the umpire’s discretion, a first breaking ball draws a warning and notice to the coach; a second breaking ball results in the pitcher being removed from the pitching position for the remainder of the game.

7.4 Mound Visits Division Rule

The coaching staff may make one mound visit per pitcher per inning without removing the pitcher. A second visit to the same pitcher in one inning requires removal from the contest. A player removed as a pitcher may not pitch again in the same game.

7.5 Intentional Walks Division Rule

Intentional walks are not permitted. At the umpire’s discretion: first offense is a warning; second offense results in the pitcher being removed from the pitcher’s position and the count to the batter reset.

7.6 Balks Division Rule

Balks may be called at the discretion of the umpires.

8. Base Running
8.1 Leads Allowed Division Rule

The base runners may take leads. A base runner may advance to any base, including home, before a pitched ball crosses home plate.

8.2 Infield Fly Rule Division Rule

The infield fly rule will be in effect.

8.3 Offensive Interference

If an offensive player deliberately runs into a defensive player in an attempt to cause the defensive player to drop the ball, the offensive player causing the interference is called out, and the remaining runners are returned to their previous bases.

8.4 Obstruction

If a defensive player blocks the base or impedes the base runner without possession of the ball, or while not in the process of receiving the ball, the runner is awarded the base to which the runner was proceeding.

8.5 Catcher Blocking the Plate

The catcher may not block home plate without the ball in his possession.

8.6 Head-First Slides

A runner who makes a head-first slide is called out, except if the slide is made to return to a base the runner has reached safely.

8.7 Slide to Avoid Contact

Whenever a defensive player is about to receive the ball or has the ball and is waiting to make a tag at a base, all base runners must slide to avoid physical contact. Any runner violating this rule is called out on the play.

8.8 Dead Ball on Timeout

Play will be dead when the umpire(s) calls or signals timeout.

8.9 Dropped Third Strike Division Rule

A base runner may not advance safely to first base when a catcher drops a 3rd strike pitch.

9. Substitutions & Roster
9.1 Intent

It is the intent of RAMMS to avoid loss of play caused by lack of players.

9.2 Minimum Players & Forfeit Division Rule

Any team fielding less than six (6) of its regular players at game time will forfeit the game. A team must field a team with a minimum of eight (8) players.

9.3 Roster Fill from American League Division Rule

A team may fill their roster with substitute RAMMS American League players for regular season games and playoff games.

9.4 Substitute Players

Substitute RAMMS players play in the outfield and bat last. If a roster player appears and more than the required players are available, the substitute is replaced by the roster player and immediately removed from the game, with the roster player taking the substitute’s spot in the batting order. A substitute may be called up by a particular team only once per season and is subject to the Commissioner’s approval.

10. Run Limit
10.1 Six-Run Rule

The offensive team may score six (6) runs in any inning, or enough runs to take a six (6) run lead if trailing at the start of their offensive half of the inning. If the score is tied or the offense is ahead, they may only score six runs per inning; if behind, they may score as many runs as needed to take a six-run lead. If the offense has one or more runners on base and the conclusion of a play from a batted ball (per Section 8) results in more than six runs or more than a six-run lead, all runs count.

11. Playoffs
11.1 Conduct of Playoffs

Playoffs are conducted pursuant to RAMMS Board Policy.

11.2 Minimum Playoff Innings

During the playoffs, at least six (6) innings are played regardless of time.

12. Eligibility & Tryouts
12.1 One League

A child shall only play in one league.

12.2 Playing Up or Down Division Rule

A player may play up or down in the next higher or lower league among the National League, American League, and Ranch League. To play down in a lower league (outside his assigned class group), the player must obtain the approval of the lower League Commissioner. To play up, the player need not seek the lower Commissioner’s approval, but the coaches and Commissioner of the upper league decide at the draft whether the player is of sufficient talent to play in the upper league safely; if not, the player is not drafted and may then play in the lower league.

12.3 Tryout Requirement

A player wanting to play in an upper league must attend the tryouts for BOTH the lower league and the upper league. League Commissioners may reassign a player who does not attend both tryouts and may grant special exemption due to special circumstances.
