RAMMS Baseball Rules

Farm League

Applies to all divisions
Specific to this division
1. Field & Equipment
1.1 Base Paths & Pitching Distance Division Rule

Base path: 45 ft.  ·  Pitching distance: 20–35 ft. (the pitching coach pitches from this range at his/her discretion).

1.2 Game Ball Division Rule

Balls will be soft T-Balls (e.g., Rawlings Soft T-Balls) or the equivalent.

1.3 Bats Division Rule

All wood bats are permitted. Non-wood bats are permitted and may have a barrel size up to two and five-eighth inches (2 5/8″) provided any 2 5/8″ bats are stamped under current USA Bat standards. All other non-wood bats, including those stamped under the USSSA standard, are not permitted.

1.4 Governing Rules Reference

Games are played according to the Official Rules of Baseball, except as modified below. The RAMMS Code of Conduct is incorporated herein.

2. Before the Game
2.1 Home Team & Dugout

The Home Team will maintain the official scorebook and will occupy the third base dugout.

2.2 Warm-ups

The Home Team may take the field thirty (30) minutes before game time for a fifteen (15) minute infield and outfield warm-up. The Visiting Team may take the field fifteen (15) minutes before game time for a fifteen (15) minute warm-up. No live batting practice is allowed on the field.

2.3 Game Balls & Score Reporting

Each coach must maintain a copy of the rules at every game. Each team contributes one (1) new ball and one back-up ball to the umpire as the game balls. The Home Team enters the final score in their scorebook and reports it in Sports Engine within forty-eight (48) hours.

3. Game Format
3.1 Innings Division Rule

A game consists of four (4) innings and no extra innings are played (except for playoffs/championship).

3.2 Time Limit Division Rule

A new inning will not start if one (1) hour has expired. An inning starts immediately after the third out or the eighth (8th) batter of the preceding inning.

3.3 Completing Innings

All innings started prior to the time limit will be completed. There is no drop-dead time. If the game is concluded due to no lights, the score reverts to the last completed inning (no makeup innings).

3.4 Official Game Clock

The umpire maintains an official game clock and the home team’s official scorekeeper should record the time of the first pitch in the official scorebook. Any intentional “delays” or “speed-ups” to circumvent the time limit may result in forfeiture at the umpire’s discretion.

3.5 Lights-Out Curfew

All play must stop no later than 8:45 p.m. (M–TR) and 9:45 p.m. (Fri–Sat). In this event the score reverts to the last completed inning (no makeup innings).

4. Participation & Playing Time
4.1 Defensive Participation Division Rule

It is the policy of RAMMS that all players have the opportunity to play at least three of the four innings of every game in the field. All players must play at least every other inning in the field on defense, disciplinary action notwithstanding.

5. Batting
5.1 Batting Order

Batting is on a bat-a-round basis. All players on the roster who appear for the game and who are not injured are listed in the batting order and given the opportunity to bat in turn.

5.2 Late Arrivals

If a player arrives late, their name is added to the bottom of the batting order.

5.3 Injured Batters

If a player is injured and cannot continue, their position in the batting order is skipped without penalty to the offensive team. The player may re-enter the game but must resume the place in the batting order he occupied before the injury.

5.4 Pitches & Tee Rescue Division Rule

Each batter receives up to five (5) pitches with no balls or strikes counted, regardless of whether or not the batter swings. If a batter does not hit a ball into the field of play after five (5) pitches, a batting tee is placed on home plate and the batter hits the ball off the tee into the field of play (“tee rescue”).

5.5 No Bunting

Players are not allowed to bunt.

5.6 Inning-Ending Conditions Division Rule

Teams bat in order until 3 outs are recorded, 4 runs are scored, or until the lineup returns to the first batter of the inning – whichever comes first. The offense may score more than four (4) runs if the completion of a play (per Section 8) leads to more than four runs – for example, with 3 runs scored and runners on base, a ball hit into the outfield is played out and all runs count.

6. Fielding
6.1 Defensive Alignment Division Rule

Teams field players at the normal infield positions (1B, 2B, SS, 3B, and P), with the exception of catcher. Any remaining players may play in the outfield at the coach’s discretion, provided all outfield players are positioned behind the infield dirt.

6.2 Overthrows Out of Play

When a thrown ball is (i) thrown or deflected outside the playing area, (ii) thrown over the outfield fence, or (iii) into the stands or dugout, all runners are entitled to advance one additional base.

7. Pitching
7.1 Coach Pitch Division Rule

Coaches pitch to their own teams for all games. The pitching coach pitches from 20–35 feet from home plate at his/her discretion. RAMMS encourages coaches to pitch the ball as flat (parallel to the ground) as possible.

7.2 Pitch Limit & Tee Rescue Division Rule

A batter receives a maximum of five (5) pitches with no balls or strikes counted per at bat. A batter receives a “tee rescue” if he/she is unable to hit a ball into the field of play within five (5) pitches.

7.3 No Walks Division Rule

All walks, intentional or otherwise, are prohibited.

7.4 Player-Pitcher Position

Prior to the ball being pitched, the pitcher player shall not be any closer to home plate than the pitching coach, with one foot on the nine (9) foot circle line to the first base or third base side of the pitching coach.

7.5 Batted Ball Striking the Pitching Coach

If the umpire determines a batted ball that strikes the pitching coach (buckets, lids, etc. are part of the coach) was unavoidable, the play is dead, the batter is awarded first base, and all other base runners advance one base. If the umpire determines the contact was avoidable or no effort was made to avoid it, play is ruled “no pitch” (not a strike, not a ball, and not counted as one of the 5 pitches) and all runners return to the base from where they began the play.

7.6 Thrown Ball Striking the Pitching Coach

Any defensive play on the third base side of the pitching coach that results in a throw unavoidably striking the pitching coach (who remains upright or in the line of the throw) results in the base runners remaining in their pre-play positions and the batter being deemed out.

7.7 Pitching Coach Conduct

The pitching coach will not field the position and will do his/her best to avoid any balls hit in his/her direction.

8. Base Running
8.1 No Stealing or Leading Off Division Rule

Bases may not be stolen. A base runner may not leave the base until the pitched ball has been hit by the batter; leaving early results in the runner being called out after one warning. A runner on third base may advance home only after (i) a hit, (ii) a play being made on the runner at third, or (iii) an overthrow of any base with the ball advancing into the outfield, along the fence, or out of play.

8.2 Infield Fly Rule

The infield fly rule will not be in effect.

8.3 Offensive Interference

If an offensive player deliberately runs into a defensive player in an attempt to cause the defensive player to drop the ball, the offensive player is called out, the play is dead, the hitter is awarded first base, and the runner is called out.

8.4 Obstruction

If a defensive player blocks the base or impedes the base runner without possession of the ball, or while not in the process of receiving the ball, the runner is awarded the base to which the runner was proceeding.

8.5 Head-First Slides

A runner who makes a head-first slide is called out, except if the slide is made to return to a base the runner has reached safely.

8.6 Slide to Avoid Contact

Whenever a defensive player is about to receive the ball or has the ball and is waiting to make a tag at a base, all base runners must slide to avoid physical contact. Any runner violating this rule is called out.

8.7 Stopping Runner Advancement (Infield Control) Division Rule

Advancement is stopped when an infielder has possession of the ball in hand or glove and is positioned inside the cut (in the dirt); if there is no dirt, then the real or imaginary lines connecting the bases (the umpire’s judgment governs). A runner may still be put out while advancing or retreating, with no risk of error by the defense. A runner past the midpoint between bases may advance only as far as the next base past midpoint, at his own risk; a runner not past the midpoint may not advance to the next base past midpoint. In any event, a runner is allowed to advance one base from an original starting point regardless of infield control. Because Farm League is meant to build a foundation for young players, on any ball hit into the outfield the offensive coach uses best judgment on advancing runners so as not to take advantage of inexperienced players.

8.8 Ball Striking Coach or Bucket

A thrown ball that hits the pitching coach or bucket is treated as a dead ball, and all runners are entitled to proceed to the base to which they were running at the time the ball hit the coach or bucket.

8.9 Hash Marks

Hash marks are placed midway between first and second, second and third, and third and home, to help the umpire decide whether a base runner is past the halfway mark and deserves to advance.

9. Substitutions & Roster
9.1 Intent

It is the intent of RAMMS to avoid loss of play caused by lack of players.

9.2 Minimum Players Division Rule

Any team fielding less than eight (8) of its regular players at game time will be rescheduled. If the game cannot be rescheduled due to conflicts, it will be forfeited by the offending team.

9.3 Substitute Players

Substitute RAMMS players play in the outfield and bat last. If a roster player appears and more than the required players are available, the substitute is replaced by the roster player and immediately removed from the game, with the roster player taking the substitute’s spot in the batting order. A substitute player is subject to the Commissioner’s approval.

10. Run Limit
10.1 Four-Run Rule Division Rule

Teams bat until 3 outs, 4 runs scored, or the lineup returns to the first batter of the inning. The offense may score more than four (4) runs only if the completion of a play leads to more than four runs (e.g., with 3 runs in and runners on base, a ball hit into the outfield is played out per Section 8 and all runs count).

11. Playoffs
11.1 Conduct of Playoffs

Playoffs are conducted pursuant to RAMMS Board Policy.

11.2 Playoff Format & Tiebreaker Division Rule

During the playoffs, at least four (4) innings are played regardless of time. In the regular season there are no extra innings; games tied at the end of regulation are recorded as a tie. In playoff/championship games tied at the end of 4 innings, teams place a runner on 2nd base and start their half of the inning with two outs; play continues in this manner until there is a winner.

12. Eligibility & Tryouts
12.1 One League

A child shall only play in one league.

12.2 Pre-School Eligibility Division Rule

A pre-school child may play in the Farm League provided the child attends the Farm League tryout and obtains special permission from the Farm League Commissioner and Farm League coaches.

12.3 Playing Up

A player wanting to play in an upper league must attend the tryouts for BOTH the lower league and the upper league. League Commissioners may reassign a player who does not attend both tryouts and may grant special exemption due to special circumstances.
