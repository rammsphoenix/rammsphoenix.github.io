# RAMMS Basketball Rules: Common to All Divisions

**1. Evaluations/Draft:** All athletes are expected to attend evaluations unless they make advance arrangements with the Director for a separate evaluation process. If the child of a coach does not go to evaluations or make separate arrangements, that child is automatically the coach's first-round draft pick. Any other athlete who does not go to evaluations and is not separately ranked cannot be drafted and is randomly assigned to a team at the end of the draft.
**2. Coaching:** The Director or League Commissioner selects coaches at their discretion, weighing volunteer service to RAMMS, past complaints, rule compliance, and RAMMS' growth and retention goals. All coaches undergo a background check, and RAMMS may remove a coach at any time for noncompliance, Code of Conduct violations, or other reasons at its discretion.
**3. Uniforms:** The "home" team should wear white or lighter jerseys, and the "away" team should wear darker jerseys.
**4. Pre-Game Discussion:** Coaches are encouraged to have a coaches' meeting with the officials before the start of every game to address potential issues and work cooperatively toward a fair, fun game for all athletes.
**5. Time:** Games consist of four 10-minute running quarters, each divided into two 5-minute periods. There is a 60-second official timeout at the 5-minute mark of each quarter for substitutions only. The clock runs continuously except for timeouts; injuries; and all free throws and whistles during the last two minutes of the second half and overtime.
**6. Scorekeeper and Scoreboard Operator:** The home team provides an adult scorekeeper, who keeps score in the scorebook and tracks the number of periods each player plays. The visiting team provides an adult scoreboard/clock operator. In the regular season only, if both head coaches agree, they may use minors instead of adults for these roles; adults are required for all playoff games.
**7. Play:** Each team has five players on the court at one time; teams play five-on-five.
**8. Attendance:** To be eligible to play, a player must be on the bench for the start of the third quarter. A player arriving after the start of the third quarter may not play because that player can no longer play the minimum of four periods.
**9. Player Call-ups:** A team forfeits (and the teams scrimmage) if it cannot field at least four of its own players within five minutes of the scheduled start time. During the regular season, a team with four of its own players may call up up to two substitutes from the league directly below; a team with five of its own players may call up one. A substitute may be called up by a particular team only once per season, and the Director of Basketball Operations or League Commissioner must approve the call-up before the player can play. Call-ups are not allowed in the playoffs.
**10. Timeouts:** In the regular season, each team gets one 60-second timeout in the first half, one in the second half, and one per overtime. In the playoffs, each team gets two per half and one per overtime. This is separate from the 60-second substitution timeout at the 5-minute mark of each quarter.
**11. Substitutions and Playing Time:** A 60-second official timeout is called at the 5-minute mark of each quarter for substitutions only (coaching kept to a minimum; purpose is to match up players). Substitutions are otherwise only permitted at the end of each quarter, except for injury or foul disqualifications. If a team has fewer players (and thus a higher maximum periods allowed), the opponent with the lower maximum may designate two players to play the opponent's higher maximum, provided everyone else plays the minimum for the team's number of players (e.g., Team A has 6 players/max 7, Team B has 8 players/max 6; Team B may designate two players to play 7 periods if all other Team B players play 4). Violations must be raised when they occur and addressed between coaches; the resolution is that the athlete sits out the period and does not exceed the maximum periods.
**12. Change of Possession:** Possession alternates at the end of each quarter, each half, and on jump balls. The team that does not win the opening jump ball is entitled to the first alternating possession. Possession does not change at the 5-minute substitution timeouts; the ball stays with the team that had possession when the timeout was called.
**13. Fouls:** A player is disqualified from the game following a fifth personal foul.
**14. Technical Fouls:** A technical foul is charged as a personal foul and results in two points awarded, except in the last two minutes of the second half/overtime, when it results in a stopped clock and two free throws. Any player, coach, or spectator receiving two technical fouls is ejected and must leave the gym area, and at a minimum is suspended for the next game. Technical fouls called for illegal defense do not count as personal fouls and do not count toward the two technicals that lead to an ejection.
**15. Injury and Disqualification Substitutions:** If a player is injured, a coach may substitute for them; if the injured player can return that same period, they may return at the first available dead ball. A partial period played by an injured player counts as a full period toward both maximum and minimum required play. A partial period played by a substitute counts toward the substitute's maximum allowed periods but not toward their minimum required periods (e.g., a player required to play 4 periods who played 3 plus a partial sub period still needs one more full period; a player with a max of 6 who played 5 plus a partial has used the 6th period). A player who has reached the maximum may not enter as a substitute. The same treatment applies to a substitute for a fouled-out or ejected player. If an injury or disqualification occurs early, coaches are encouraged to agree on adjusted requirements; coaches may not encourage a player to purposely foul out or get injured to avoid maximum playing-time requirements.
**16. Overtime:** Regular-season games tied at the end of regulation play a two-minute overtime (last-two-minutes rules apply); if still tied, the game is scored as a tie. Playoff overtime is five minutes, with successive two-minute periods until the tie is broken. Playing-time requirements do not apply in overtime, and each coach may put any five players on the floor.
**17. Safety:** Basketball shoes must be worn on the court. No watches, necklaces, or personal jewelry may be worn during play. No casts (soft or hard) are permitted under any circumstances. No spectators are allowed on the court during the game.
**18. Rules:** Unless a specific RAMMS Basketball rule applies, RAMMS follows the current edition of the National Federation Basketball Rules Book.
**19. Ejections:** Any player, coach, or spectator ejected by the referee for any reason must immediately leave the area and is automatically suspended for the next game, regular season or postseason. All ejections are reviewed by the RAMMS D&R Committee for possible Code of Conduct violations and further discipline.
**20. Games Are Final:** Once a game is called final by the official, RAMMS will not change the outcome. Coaches should raise issues with the official when they occur. The RAMMS board may forfeit any team for willful noncompliance or other misconduct.
**21. Practices:** A RAMMS-approved Head or Assistant Coach must be present at every practice, game, or team event; none may be conducted without one. If a coach cannot make a scheduled game, contact the Director of Boys or Girls Basketball or the league Commissioner.
**22. Playoff Tiebreakers:** Playoff rankings are based on winning percentage. Ties are broken in order by: (1) head-to-head; (2) record vs. common opponents; (3) coin flip (or random number generator if more than two teams remain tied).
**23. Player Time Requirements:** For five-on-five play, minimums/maximums depend on players present: 9, 8, or 7 players present — minimum 4 periods, maximum 6; 6 players present — minimum 6, maximum 7; 5 players present — all 8 periods. (Identical across divisions.)
**24. Offensive Limitations:** A team may not implement a four-corner or isolation offense in which the majority of offensive players are stationed in the corners or perimeter of the court, except during the final thirty seconds of a period when holding the ball for a final shot or stalling. Enforcement: the first violation in each half is a warning; each subsequent violation per half is a technical foul.
**25. Defensive Violation Enforcement:** Teams receive two warnings per half for each defensive rule; each subsequent violation of a particular rule, per half, results in a technical foul.
**26. Free Throw Stepping:** Players may pass over (cross) the foul line while shooting a free throw without penalty. The free throw line is marked with tape before the game or otherwise established by the referees.
**27. Shooting Foul Penalties:** Outside the last two minutes, fouls in the act of shooting award points plus the ball (see division pages for the exact points). In the last two minutes of the second half and overtime, any defensive shooting or non-shooting foul results in a stopped clock and free throws. Offensive fouls result in a change of possession.
**28. Back Court Defense:** Except in the last two minutes of the second half and in overtime, a team may not play defense in the backcourt (past the division-specific boundary) once the opponent gains control of the ball. In the last two minutes of the second half and overtime, full-court man-to-man is allowed (with double-team allowances varying by division — see division pages); all other defenses remain prohibited.
