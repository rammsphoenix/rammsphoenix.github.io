# RAMMS Basketball Rules: Common to All Divisions

**1. Evaluations/Draft:** All athletes must attend evaluations or make arrangements with the Director in advance; otherwise, a coach's child is automatically the first round pick, and unranked athletes are randomly assigned at the end of the draft [1].
**2. Coaching:** The Director or League Commissioner selects coaches at their discretion, weighing volunteer service to RAMMS, past complaints, rule compliance, and RAMMS’ growth and retention goals. All coaches undergo a background check, and RAMMS may remove a coach at any time for noncompliance, Code of Conduct violations, or other reasons at its discretion.
**3. Uniforms:** The "home" team should wear white or lighter jerseys, and the "away" team should wear darker jerseys [2].
**4. Pre-Game Discussion:** Coaches are encouraged to hold a pre-game meeting with officials to prevent issues and ensure a fair game [2].
**5. Time:** Games consist of four 10-minute running quarters, divided into two 5-minute periods [2]. The clock runs continuously except for timeouts, injuries, and all free throws and whistles in the last two minutes of the second half and overtime [2].
**6. Scorekeeper and Scoreboard Operator:** The home team provides an adult scorekeeper, and the visiting team provides an adult scoreboard/clock operator [3]. Minors can be used in the regular season if both head coaches agree, but adults are required for the playoffs [3].
**7. Play:** Teams play five-on-five [4].
**8. Attendance:** A player must be on the bench by the start of the third quarter to be eligible to play [4].
**9. Player Call-ups:** A team forfeits if it cannot field at least four of its own players within five minutes of the start time [4]. During the regular season, teams with four players can call up two substitutes from the league directly below, and teams with five can call up one substitute [4]. Substitutes can only be called up once per season and require approval [4]. Call-ups are not allowed in the playoffs [4].
**10. Timeouts:** In the regular season, teams get one 60-second timeout per half and one per overtime [5]. In the playoffs, teams get two per half and one per overtime [5].
**11. Substitutions and Playing Time:** Substitutions are only allowed at the 5-minute mark of each quarter and at the end of quarters, except for injury or foul disqualifications [5]. If a team has a roster size advantage, the opponent can designate up to two players to match the higher maximum periods allowed, provided everyone else meets minimum play requirements [6]. Playing time violations require the player to sit out the period [7].
**12. Change of Possession:** Possession alternates at the end of quarters, halves, and jump balls, but not during 5-minute substitution timeouts [7].
**13. Fouls:** Players are disqualified after their fifth personal foul [8].
**14. Technical Fouls:** Technical fouls count as personal fouls and award two points, except in the last two minutes of the second half/overtime when they result in a stopped clock and two free throws [8]. Two technicals result in an ejection and a minimum suspension for the next game [8]. Illegal defense technicals do not count towards ejections [8].
**15. Injury and Disqualification Substitutions:** Partial periods played by an injured player count as a full period for both maximum and minimum requirements [9]. Partial periods played by a substitute count toward their maximum allowed periods, but not their minimum required periods [9-11].
**16. Overtime:** Regular season games tied at the end of regulation have a two-minute overtime, and end in a tie if still unresolved [11]. Playoff overtime is five minutes, with successive two-minute periods until a winner is determined [11]. Playing time requirements do not apply in overtime [11].
**17. Safety:** Basketball shoes are mandatory, and jewelry, watches, and casts are strictly prohibited [12]. Spectators are not allowed on the court [12].
**18. Rules:** The current National Federation Basketball Rules Book applies where specific RAMMS rules do not exist [12].
**19. Ejections:** Any ejected player, coach, or spectator must leave the area immediately and is suspended for the next game [12].
**20. Games Are Final:** RAMMS will not change the outcome of a game once it is called final by the official. Coaches should raise issues with the official when they occur, and the RAMMS board reserves the right to forfeit any team for willful noncompliance with the rules or other misconduct [13].
**21. Practices:** A RAMMS approved Head or Assistant Coach must be present at every practice, game, or team event. If a coach cannot make a scheduled game, contact the Director of Boys or Girls Basketball or the league Commissioner to discuss solutions [13].
**22. Playoff Tiebreakers:** Rankings use winning percentage, followed by head-to-head records, common opponents, and a coin flip/random number generator [14].
**23. Player Time Requirements:** Standardized minimum and maximum periods are identical across all divisions, based on the number of players present (e.g., 9 players must play a minimum of 4 periods and maximum of 6) [14].
**24. Offensive Limitations:** Four corner or isolation offenses are prohibited except during the last thirty seconds of a period [15].
**25. Defensive Violation Enforcement:** Teams receive two warnings per half for defensive violations, with subsequent violations resulting in a technical foul [16].
**26. Free Throw Stepping:** Players may cross the foul line while shooting without penalty [16].
**27. Shooting Foul Penalties:** Outside the last two minutes, shooting fouls result in points plus the ball [16]. Inside the last two minutes of the second half and overtime, fouls result in a stopped clock and free throws [16, 17].
**28. Back Court Defense:** Backcourt defense is strictly prohibited during regular play across all divisions, and is only permitted in the last two minutes of the second half and overtime [17].
