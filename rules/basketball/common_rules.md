# RAMMS Basketball Rules: Common to All Divisions

**1. Evaluations/Draft:** All athletes must attend evaluations or make arrangements with the Director in advance; otherwise, a coach's child is automatically the first round pick, and unranked athletes are randomly assigned at the end of the draft [1].
**2. Uniforms:** The "home" team should wear white or lighter jerseys, and the "away" team should wear darker jerseys [2].
**3. Pre-Game Discussion:** Coaches are encouraged to hold a pre-game meeting with officials to prevent issues and ensure a fair game [2].
**4. Time:** Games consist of four 10-minute running quarters, divided into two 5-minute periods [2]. The clock runs continuously except for timeouts, injuries, and all free throws and whistles in the last two minutes of the second half and overtime [2].
**5. Scorekeeper and Scoreboard Operator:** The home team provides an adult scorekeeper, and the visiting team provides an adult scoreboard/clock operator [3]. Minors can be used in the regular season if both head coaches agree, but adults are required for the playoffs [3].
**6. Play:** Teams play five-on-five [4].
**7. Attendance:** A player must be on the bench by the start of the third quarter to be eligible to play [4].
**8. Player Call-ups:** A team forfeits if it cannot field at least four of its own players within five minutes of the start time [4]. During the regular season, teams with four players can call up two substitutes from the league directly below, and teams with five can call up one substitute [4]. Substitutes can only be called up once per season and require approval [4]. Call-ups are not allowed in the playoffs [4].
**9. Timeouts:** In the regular season, teams get one 60-second timeout per half and one per overtime [5]. In the playoffs, teams get two per half and one per overtime [5].
**10. Substitutions and Playing Time:** Substitutions are only allowed at the 5-minute mark of each quarter and at the end of quarters, except for injury or foul disqualifications [5]. If a team has a roster size advantage, the opponent can designate up to two players to match the higher maximum periods allowed, provided everyone else meets minimum play requirements [6]. Playing time violations require the player to sit out the period [7].
**11. Change of Possession:** Possession alternates at the end of quarters, halves, and jump balls, but not during 5-minute substitution timeouts [7].
**12. Fouls:** Players are disqualified after their fifth personal foul [8].
**13. Technical Fouls:** Technical fouls count as personal fouls and award two points, except in the last two minutes of the second half/overtime when they result in a stopped clock and two free throws [8]. Two technicals result in an ejection and a minimum suspension for the next game [8]. Illegal defense technicals do not count towards ejections [8].
**14. Injury and Disqualification Substitutions:** Partial periods played by an injured player count as a full period for both maximum and minimum requirements [9]. Partial periods played by a substitute count toward their maximum allowed periods, but not their minimum required periods [9-11].
**15. Overtime:** Regular season games tied at the end of regulation have a two-minute overtime, and end in a tie if still unresolved [11]. Playoff overtime is five minutes, with successive two-minute periods until a winner is determined [11]. Playing time requirements do not apply in overtime [11].
**16. Safety:** Basketball shoes are mandatory, and jewelry, watches, and casts are strictly prohibited [12]. Spectators are not allowed on the court [12].
**17. Rules:** The current National Federation Basketball Rules Book applies where specific RAMMS rules do not exist [12].
**18. Ejections:** Any ejected player, coach, or spectator must leave the area immediately and is suspended for the next game [12].
**19. Games Are Final:** RAMMS will not change the outcome of a game once it is called final by the official [13].
**20. Practices:** A RAMMS approved Head or Assistant Coach must be present at every practice, game, or team event [13].
**21. Playoff Tiebreakers:** Rankings use winning percentage, followed by head-to-head records, common opponents, and a coin flip/random number generator [14].
**22. Player Time Requirements:** Standardized minimum and maximum periods are identical across all divisions, based on the number of players present (e.g., 9 players must play a minimum of 4 periods and maximum of 6) [14].
**23. Offensive Limitations:** Four corner or isolation offenses are prohibited except during the last thirty seconds of a period [15].
**24. Defensive Violation Enforcement:** Teams receive two warnings per half for defensive violations, with subsequent violations resulting in a technical foul [16].
**25. Free Throw Stepping:** Players may cross the foul line while shooting without penalty [16].
**26. Shooting Foul Penalties:** Outside the last two minutes, shooting fouls result in points plus the ball [16]. Inside the last two minutes of the second half and overtime, fouls result in a stopped clock and free throws [16, 17].
**27. Back Court Defense:** Backcourt defense is strictly prohibited during regular play across all divisions, and is only permitted in the last two minutes of the second half and overtime [17].
