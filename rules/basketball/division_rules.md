# RAMMS Basketball Rules: Division-Specific Rules

**1. Hoop Height, Ball Size, and Free Throw Distance:**
- **Boys K & Girls K-1st:** 8-foot hoop, 25.5" ball, ~10-foot free throw line.
- **Boys 1st-2nd & Girls 2nd-3rd:** 8-foot hoop, 27.5" ball, ~13-foot free throw line.
- **Boys 3rd-4th & Girls 4th-5th:** 9-foot hoop, 28.5" ball, ~13-foot free throw line.
- **Boys 5th-8th:** 10-foot hoop, 29.5" ball, standard 15-foot free throw line.
- **Girls 6th-8th:** 10-foot hoop, 28.5" ball, standard 15-foot free throw line.

**2. Lane Violations:**
- **Boys K, Girls K-1st, & Girls 2nd-3rd:** No lane (three-second) violation is enforced.
- **Boys 1st-2nd, Boys 3rd-4th, & Girls 4th-5th:** Lane violation if inside the key for five seconds.
- **Boys 5th-8th & Girls 6th-8th:** Lane violation if inside the key for three seconds.

**3. Points Awarded for Shooting Fouls (Outside Last 2 Minutes):**
- **Boys K, Boys 1st-2nd, Boys 3rd-4th, Girls K-1st, Girls 2nd-3rd, Girls 4th-5th:** 1 point plus the ball for fouls committed in the act of shooting (any shot).
- **Boys 5th-8th & Girls 6th-8th:** 1 point plus the ball for fouls on two-point shots; 2 points plus the ball for fouls on three-point shots.
- In the last two minutes of the second half and overtime, any defensive shooting or non-shooting foul results in a stopped clock and free throws (two free throws in divisions with no three-point distinction; two or three free throws — depending on the shot — in Boys 3rd-4th, Girls 4th-5th, Boys 5th-8th, and Girls 6th-8th). Offensive fouls result in a change of possession.

**4. Defensive Limitations:**
- **Boys K / Girls K-1st:** Teams must play man-to-man; no zone, no double team (inside or outside the paint), and no backcourt defense except as permitted in 5.3. Players may NOT steal (from their own man or anyone) and may NOT swipe. Players may switch on a pick but must switch back at the first opportunity. Players may intercept passes and may try to block a shot when the player they are guarding shoots. Defenders may sag off their man (no more than three feet when the offensive player is inside the three-point area; more when outside) — the Boys K page states this sag rule explicitly, while the Girls K-1st page omits the sag distances in the source.
- **Boys 1st-2nd:** Man-to-man; no zone, no double team outside the paint. Sag off the man (no more than three feet inside the three-point area; more outside). Players may steal from their own man and may swipe when the dribbler initiates the action. Players may switch on a pick and switch back. Double team permitted only in the paint, and only when the offensive player is entering or in the paint with the ball; in the paint, a player may steal from any player, swipe at any time, and block any shot.
- **Boys 3rd-4th, Girls 2nd-3rd, Girls 4th-5th:** Man-to-man; no zone, no double team outside the paint. Weak-side defenders (two passes away) may not sag into the paint unless their man is within three feet of the paint; strong-side defenders (one pass away) may sag (no more than three feet inside the three-point area; more outside). Players may steal from their own man and may swipe when the dribbler initiates. Switching: Boys 3rd-4th and Girls 2nd-3rd may switch on a pick; Girls 4th-5th may switch provided only one player guards the other at a time outside the paint. Double team permitted only in the paint, and only when the offensive player is entering or in the paint with the ball; in the paint, a player may steal from any player, swipe at any time, and block any shot.
- **Boys 5th-8th & Girls 6th-8th:** Teams may play three-quarter-court man-to-man defense (starting from the top-of-key extended across the court), one-quarter-court zone defense (starting from the top-of-key extended across the court), and may double team inside the top-of-key extended across the court. Steals, swipes, and blocks on any player are permitted anywhere legal defense is played.

**5. Backcourt Defense Areas:**
- **Boys K / Girls K-1st:** No backcourt defense during regular play. In the last two minutes/overtime, full-court man-to-man is allowed but double-teaming is NOT permitted; all other defenses remain prohibited.
- **Boys 1st-2nd, Boys 3rd-4th, Girls 2nd-3rd, Girls 4th-5th:** No backcourt defense during regular play. In the last two minutes/overtime, full-court man-to-man is allowed and the team may double team the ball; all other defenses remain prohibited.
- **Boys 5th-8th / Girls 6th-8th:** No defense past the top-of-key extended into the backcourt during regular play. In the last two minutes/overtime, full-court man-to-man with double-teaming the ball is allowed; all other full-court defenses remain prohibited.

**6. Wristband Matchup System:**
- **Boys K, Boys 1st-2nd, Girls K-1st, & Girls 2nd-3rd:** All players wear a colored wristband when in the game; one team puts on wristbands first each period and the other matches up. The wristband order (provided by the Director of Boys/Girls Basketball or League Commissioner) is alphabetical by color from strongest to weakest player; RAMMS may use two wristbands of the same color for the strongest players. Coaches must match better players against the opponent's better players and must not create mismatches; disputes are resolved by the referees, who report the coaches to the Director of Basketball Operations. A player may only guard the opponent with the same colored wristband, except to stop a fast break or on a switch on a pick (the no-double-team rules still apply).
- **Boys 3rd-4th, Boys 5th-8th, Girls 4th-5th, Girls 6th-8th:** No wristbands are used.
