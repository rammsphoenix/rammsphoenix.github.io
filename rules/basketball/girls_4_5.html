RAMMS Basketball Rules

Girls 4th–5th Grade

Applies to all divisions
Specific to this division
1. Equipment & Court Setup
1.1 Hoop Height, Ball Size & Free Throw Line Division Rule

9-foot hoop  ·  28.5″ ball  ·  ~13-foot free throw line

2. Before the Game
2.1 Evaluations & Draft

All athletes must attend evaluations or make arrangements with the Director in advance. Otherwise, a coach’s child is automatically the first-round pick, and unranked athletes are randomly assigned at the end of the draft.

2.2 Uniforms

The “home” team wears white or lighter jerseys; the “away” team wears darker jerseys.

2.3 Pre-Game Discussion

Coaches are encouraged to hold a pre-game meeting with officials to prevent issues and ensure a fair game.

2.4 Scorekeeper & Scoreboard Operator

The home team provides an adult scorekeeper; the visiting team provides an adult scoreboard/clock operator. Minors may be used in the regular season if both head coaches agree, but adults are required for the playoffs.

3. Game Format
3.1 Teams

Games are played five-on-five.

3.2 Clock & Quarters

Games consist of four 10-minute running quarters, each divided into two 5-minute periods. The clock runs continuously except for timeouts, injuries, and all free throws and whistles in the last two minutes of the second half and overtime.

3.3 Timeouts

Regular season: one 60-second timeout per half, plus one per overtime period.

Playoffs: two timeouts per half, plus one per overtime period.

3.4 Change of Possession

Possession alternates at the end of quarters, halves, and jump balls, but not during 5-minute substitution timeouts.

4. Player Eligibility & Playing Time
4.1 Game-Day Attendance

A player must be on the bench by the start of the third quarter to be eligible to play.

4.2 Player Call-ups & Forfeit

A team forfeits if it cannot field at least four of its own players within five minutes of the start time. During the regular season, teams with four players may call up two substitutes from the league directly below; teams with five players may call up one. Substitutes can only be called up once per season and require approval. Call-ups are not allowed in the playoffs.

4.3 Substitutions

Substitutions are only allowed at the 5-minute mark of each quarter and at the end of quarters, except for injuries or foul disqualifications. If one team has a roster size advantage, the opposing coach may designate up to two players to match the higher maximum periods allowed, provided all other players meet minimum play requirements. Playing time violations require the affected player to sit out the period.

4.4 Playing Time Requirements

Standardized minimums and maximums apply across all divisions based on players present (e.g., 9 players: minimum 4 periods, maximum 6). Partial periods by an injured player count as a full period for both max and min. Partial periods by a substitute count toward their maximum but not their minimum. Playing time requirements do not apply in overtime.

5. Defense
5.1 Defensive Style & Limitations Division Rule

Man-to-man defense required. Steals and swipes are allowed. Double-teaming is permitted only in the paint. Weak-side sag restriction: a weak-side defender may not sag into the paint unless their man is within 3 feet of the paint.

5.2 Wristband Matchup System

Not used in this division.

5.3 Backcourt Defense Division Rule

Regular play: No defense past half court.

Last 2 minutes & overtime: Full court man-to-man is allowed. Double-teaming is permitted.

5.4 Defensive Violation Enforcement

Each team receives two warnings per half for defensive violations. Subsequent violations in the same half result in a technical foul.

6. Offense
6.1 Offensive Limitations

Four-corner or isolation offenses are prohibited except during the last thirty seconds of a period.

7. Fouls & Free Throws
7.1 Personal Fouls

Players are disqualified after their fifth personal foul.

7.2 Technical Fouls

Technical fouls count as personal fouls and award two points to the opposing team, except in the last two minutes of the second half/overtime, when they result in a stopped clock and two free throws. Two technicals result in ejection and a minimum one-game suspension. Illegal defense technicals do not count toward ejections.

7.3 Lane Violations Division Rule

5 seconds in the key.

7.4 Shooting Foul Penalties Division Rule

Outside the last 2 minutes: 1 point + possession of the ball for all shooting fouls. Note: application to 3-point shooting fouls has some ambiguity in the source rules.

Last 2 minutes & overtime: Stopped clock + free throws.

7.5 Free Throw Stepping

Players may cross the foul line while shooting without penalty.

8. Overtime
8.1 Regular Season

Tied games after regulation play a two-minute overtime period. If still tied, the game ends in a tie.

8.2 Playoffs

Playoff overtime is five minutes. If still tied, successive two-minute periods are played until a winner is determined. Playing time requirements do not apply in overtime.

9. Playoffs
9.1 Tiebreakers

Rankings are determined by: (1) winning percentage, (2) head-to-head record, (3) common opponents, (4) coin flip or random number generator.

9.2 Call-up Restrictions

Player call-ups are not permitted in the playoffs. See Section 4.2.

10. Safety & Conduct
10.1 Safety Requirements

Basketball shoes are mandatory. Jewelry, watches, and casts are strictly prohibited. Spectators are not allowed on the court.

10.2 Ejections

Any ejected player, coach, or spectator must leave the playing area immediately and is suspended for the next game.

10.3 Games Are Final

RAMMS will not change the outcome of a game once it has been called final by the official.

10.4 Coaching Requirements

A RAMMS-approved Head or Assistant Coach must be present at every practice, game, or team event.

10.5 Governing Rules Reference

The current National Federation Basketball Rules Book applies in all situations not specifically addressed by RAMMS rules.
