# RAMMS Flag Football Rules: Common to All Divisions

**1. Purpose:** The intent of the program is to provide clean, wholesome recreation for the maximum number of youngsters, helping young people learn to win and lose while preparing them for further football opportunities.
**2. Governing Rules:** Games are played according to the Official Rules of Football, except as modified by these rules and the RAMMS Code of Conduct.
**3. No Do-Overs:** Once the ball is snapped, the play has occurred and the down is counted; there is no repeat of a down unless the penalty rules allow it to be replayed. There are no "do-overs" for an error, mistake, or collision that is not deemed a penalty.
**4. Sidelines:** Teams are on separate sidelines, with the home team on the side that has the scoreboard.
**5. No Contact:** No contact is allowed, including tackling, diving, blocking, and screening. No jumping spin moves are allowed.
**6. Coaching Shirt:** The RAMMS-approved Head and Assistant Coach must wear the RAMMS Flag Football coaching shirt during the game.
**7. Responsibility for Players and Fans:** Coaches are responsible for their players' and fans' conduct before, during, and after games. Teams may be penalized with Personal Fouls / Unsportsmanlike Conduct for inappropriate bench or fan behavior, subject to review by the RAMMS D&R Committee.
**8. Communication with Referees:** Only Coaches may speak to the Referees; no other individuals may. Each team gets one warning, after which violations are penalized under the Unsportsmanlike Conduct rule.
**9. Field Dimensions:** The 5-on-5 field is approximately 50–53 yards by 30 yards (end zone to end zone, sideline to sideline), with the Mid-Field Line at 25 yards.
**10. No-Run Zones:** A No-Run Zone is marked at Mid-Field and also applies at the 5-yard line of both end zones.
**11. Flags:** Each player must wear a set of RAMMS-supplied flags at all times. No flag belt means a dead ball and the player is down where they gained possession. Tampering with the flag belt is prohibited: first infraction is a 15-yard penalty and loss of down, second awards 6 points to the opponent plus a 15-yard Unsportsmanlike penalty, loss of down, and disqualification for the rest of the game, and a third results in forfeit.
**12. Jewelry and Uniform:** Jewelry shall not be worn; religious/medical medals must be taped to the chest under the uniform. No hats (beanies and tight-fitting head gear are permitted), no pockets on shorts/pants, jerseys tucked in at all times, and tight-fitting wrist bands allowed.
**13. Cleats:** Rubber football cleats are permitted; no metal spikes.
**14. Cones and Scoreboard:** The Visiting Team places the field cones if not already placed; the Home Team maintains the official scoreboard.
**15. Scoring:** Touchdown = 6 points (scored the instant the ball crosses the goal-line plane). PAT from the 5-yard line = 1 point (pass only); PAT from the 10-yard line = 2 points (pass or run); a PAT returned by the defense for a score = 2 points. Safety = 2 points.
**16. Coin Toss and Direction:** Captains meet at midfield for the coin toss; the visiting team calls it. The winner chooses offense or defense, the loser chooses direction. The second half goes to the team that started on defense, and teams change direction.
**17. Kickoffs:** Each half begins with a kickoff. The receiving team starts where the return ends or the ball is downed; a touchback starts at the receiving team's own 5-yard line; a muffed/dead kick is spotted where first touched; an out-of-bounds kick is placed at the 10-yard line. No onside kickoffs. If the kicking team touches the ball, it is dead and the receiving team starts there.
**18. Downs:** The offense has 4 downs to cross midfield (it may go for it or punt on the 4th; a failed 4th down or a punt gives the opponent the ball, with a punt spotted at the opponent's own 5-yard line). After crossing midfield, the offense has 3 downs to score; failing that, a change of possession spots the ball at the opponent's own 5-yard line.
**19. Game Length and Timing:** Two 20-minute halves with a running clock and a 5-minute halftime. A half ends at "no time" but not on a defensive penalty (yards are assessed and another play is run). The clock stops for timeouts, the last 2 minutes of the 2nd half on out-of-bounds or incomplete passes, injuries, Delay of Game penalties, and defensive penalties.
**20. Timeouts:** Each team gets 1 timeout per half in the regular season and 2 per half in the post season.
**21. Last-Two-Minute Change of Possession:** In the last 2 minutes of the 2nd half, a change of possession stops the clock for up to 30 seconds so both teams can take the field, huddle, and call the next play.
**22. Play Clock:** The offense has 30 seconds to snap once the Referee spots the ball, with one Delay of Game warning per half per team.
**23. Overtime:** Regulation scoring (TD, PAT, safety, interceptions) applies. The visiting team calls a coin toss for first choice of offense/defense. In the 1st OT each team gets one play from the defense's 5-yard line (1 point) or 10-yard line (2 points) and may choose; from the 2nd OT on, both teams must attempt a 2-point conversion from the 10-yard line. Teams alternate offense/defense each round until a winner is decided. No timeouts; interceptions are returnable for 2 points and end the game; the Final Score includes all overtime points and the referee picks the end of the field.
**24. Players and Forfeit:** In the 5-on-5 leagues, a team must have at least 4 players from the start through completion of the game or it forfeits.
**25. Mandatory Playing Time:** All players must play at least one-half of every game in the field unless specifically authorized. Playing a player less than one-half requires discussion with and permission from the Director of Football Operations; violations cause a forfeit.
**26. Playing-Time Series Rule:** No substitutions during an Offensive or Defensive series except for injury; the players who start a series finish it. No player sits out more than 2 series in succession. First infraction is a 15-yard penalty and loss of down; the second and later infractions award 6 points to the opponent plus a 15-yard penalty and loss of down.
**27. Roster Substitutions:** A team may carry a game-day roster of one more player than required. If a roster player later appears, the substitute is replaced and is no longer eligible for that game. A substitute may be called up by a team only once per season, subject to the Director's/Commissioner's approval.
**28. Center/Quarterback Exchange:** The ball must be snapped between the legs to start play, and the Quarterback may not hand the ball back to the center between the legs immediately post-snap.
**29. Motion and Formations:** Only one player may be in motion at a time, moving parallel to the line of scrimmage; all other players must be set for 1 second at the snap.
**30. Running:** No running in No-Run Zones (5 yards from First Downs and 5 yards from End Zones). The Quarterback may not run past the Line of Scrimmage. Only direct handoffs behind the Line of Scrimmage are allowed (multiple handoffs allowed); no laterals or pitches anywhere. A player who takes a direct handoff may throw from behind the Line of Scrimmage. Spinning and lateral moves are allowed, but jump spins are not.
**31. No Flag Guarding, Blocking, or Straight Arming:** Flag guarding, blocking (other players must stop running once a teammate has the ball), and straight arming are prohibited. A diving/leaping/jumping runner who creates contact draws a personal foul.
**32. Passing:** All passes must be forward and received beyond the Line of Scrimmage (including shovel/screen passes).
**33. Receiving:** All players are eligible to receive (including the Quarterback after a handoff); a receiver must have one foot in bounds.
**34. Fumbles:** A fumble anywhere on the field is whistled dead, marked at the fumble spot, and the fumbling team retains possession.
**35. Rushing the Quarterback:** Any number may rush, but rushers must start a minimum of 10 yards from the Line of Scrimmage (marked by the Referee) at the snap. Non-rushers may defend on the line. Once across the line, a rusher cannot intentionally block the pass and must go for the flag (hands up = 15 yards plus first down). Passes may be blocked before the defender crosses the line. Rushers need not announce themselves.
**36. Rushing the Ball Carrier:** If the ball is handed off behind the line, all defenders may rush the ball carrier regardless of the line of scrimmage.
**37. Defenses Allowed:** Zone, man-to-man, or any combination is allowed. Jamming receivers or intentional contact is not allowed.
**38. Interceptions:** On an interception, the defense becomes the offense and play continues until the ball carrier's flag is pulled.
**39. Offensive Penalties:** Offensive penalties are 5 yards unless noted (illegal motion, false start, illegal forward pass, offensive pass interference, flag guarding, delay of game, straight arming*). Personal Foul = 15 yards and loss of down (two by a player = ejection); Unsportsmanlike Conduct = 15 yards and loss of down. The ball must be snapped between the Center's legs. Flag-guarding penalties are assessed from the line (no loss of down) or from the spot if the ball was advanced (no replay of the down). Penalties apply on PATs, penalties within the 5-yard line are half the distance to the goal, and penalties may be declined.
**40. Defensive Penalties:** Offside/Encroachment – 5 yards; Pass Interference – 5 yards plus first down; Illegal flag pull (before the receiver has the ball) – 5 yards plus first down; Illegal rushing (from inside the marker) – 5 yards; Contact with the Quarterback's throwing arm – 15 yards plus first down; Personal Foul – 15 yards plus first down; Unsportsmanlike Conduct – 15 yards plus first down.
**41. Playoffs:** Playoffs are conducted under RAMMS Board Policy and are single elimination.
**42. Reporting and Rules on Hand:** Each coach keeps a copy of the rules at every game. The Home Team reports the final score (division, both teams, score) to the League Commissioner within twenty-four hours, preferably immediately, via email.
**43. One League:** A child may only play in one league.
**44. Playing Up or Down:** A player may play in the next higher or lower division only with the approval of the Director of Football; to play up, the player must attend tryouts for both divisions, and the proper division is decided by the coaches' evaluation for safety.
**45. Ejections:** Any ejected player, coach, or spectator must immediately leave the field area and is automatically suspended for the next game (regular or post season); all ejections are reviewed by the RAMMS D&R Committee.
**46. Coach Presence:** A RAMMS-approved Head or Assistant Coach must be present at every practice, game, or team event, with no exceptions.
