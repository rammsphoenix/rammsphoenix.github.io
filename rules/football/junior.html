RAMMS Football Rules

Junior Division

Applies to all divisions
Specific to this division
1. Purpose
1.1 Intent of the Program

The intent of the program is to provide clean, wholesome recreation for a maximum number of youngsters. Adults who are involved have the opportunity, through their words and actions, to assist young people in learning to win, as well as lose, while preparing the youngsters for further football opportunities during their careers.

2. General Game Rules & Conduct
2.1 Governing Rules

Games are played according to the Official Rules of Football, except as modified below and the RAMMS Code of Conduct.

2.2 No “Do-Overs”

Once the ball is snapped, the play has occurred and the down is counted. There will not be a repeat of that down unless the rules of penalties allow that down to be replayed. In essence, there will not be any “do-overs” due to an error, mistake, or collision of the players, when not deemed a penalty.

2.3 Sidelines

During a game, teams shall be on separate sidelines. The home team shall be on the side which has the scoreboard.

2.4 No Contact

There’s no contact allowed. That includes tackling, diving, blocking, and screening. No jumping spin moves are allowed.

2.5 Coaching Shirt

The RAMMS-approved Head and Assistant Coach MUST wear the RAMMS Flag Football coaching shirt during the game.

2.6 Responsibility for Players & Fans

Coaches are responsible for their players AND fans’ conduct before, during and after games. Teams may be penalized with Personal Fouls / Unsportsmanlike Conduct for inappropriate behavior from their bench or fans. Unsportsmanlike Conduct penalties are subject to review by the RAMMS D&R Committee for possible Code of Conduct violations and, if warranted, further disciplinary action.

2.7 Communication with Referees

Only Coaches are authorized to speak to the Referees. No other individuals, players, parents, siblings, fans, etc. shall speak to the Referees. Each team will be given one warning. Subsequent violations will be penalized pursuant to the Unsportsmanlike Conduct rule — a 15-yard penalty and loss of down, with the conduct subject to review by the RAMMS D&R Committee.

3. Field Dimensions & Equipment
3.1 Field Dimensions

The 5-on-5 field dimensions will be approximately 50–53 yards by 30 yards (end zone to end zone, sideline to sideline). The Mid-Field Line will be at 25 yards.

3.2 No-Run Zones

A NO-RUN ZONE is marked at Mid-Field, and also applies at the 5-yard line of both end zones.

3.3 Football Size Division Rule

The Junior Division shall use a “Junior” football for all games.

3.4 Flags

Each player must have a set of flags on at all times; flags are supplied by RAMMS. Failure to wear a flag belt results in a dead ball, and a player not wearing flags is called down at the point they gained possession. Tampering with the flag belt to gain an advantage — tying, cutting, wrapping, knotting, foreign materials, or similar acts — is prohibited; the first infraction is a 15-yard penalty and loss of down, the second awards 6 points to the opposing team plus a 15-yard Unsportsmanlike penalty, loss of down, and disqualification of the player for the remainder of the game, and a third infraction results in a forfeit.

3.5 Jewelry

Jewelry shall not be worn. Religious and medical alert medals are not considered jewelry but must be taped to the player’s chest and worn under the uniform.

3.6 Uniform & Headwear

NO HATS shall be worn; beanies and tight-fitting head gear are permitted. Shorts and football pants are permitted, but NO POCKETS are allowed. The jersey must be tucked into shorts/pants at all times. Tight-fitting wrist bands are allowed.

3.7 Cleats

Rubber football cleats are permitted; no metal spikes.

3.8 Cones & Scoreboard

The Visiting Team is responsible for placing the cones on the field if they have not already been placed. The Home Team will maintain the official scoreboard.

4. Scoring
4.1 Touchdown

Touchdown = 6 pts. A touchdown is scored the instant the ball crosses the plane of the goal line.

4.2 Point After Touchdown

PAT attempted from the 5-yard line = 1 pt (Pass Only). PAT attempted from the 10-yard line = 2 pts (Pass or Run).

4.3 Safety

Safety = 2 pts.

4.4 Returned PAT

A Point After Touchdown returned for a score by the defense = 2 pts (regardless of whether the attempt was from the 5-yard or 10-yard line).

5. Game Start, Kickoffs & Downs
5.1 Coin Toss

At the start of each game, captains from both teams meet at midfield for the coin toss to determine who starts with the ball. The visiting team calls the toss. The winner has the choice of offense or defense; the loser has the choice of direction. Possession to start the second half goes to the team that started the game on defense, and the teams change direction.

5.2 Kickoffs

At the beginning of each half, the ball is kicked off, and possession begins with a kickoff. The receiving team begins its offensive possession where the return ends or where the ball is downed. If the ball is kicked into the end zone and not returned, the receiving team starts at its own 5-yard line. If the kick is muffed and declared dead, the ball is spotted where first touched. If kicked out of bounds, it is placed at the 10-yard line. There are no onside kickoffs. If the kicking team touches the ball, it is blown dead and the receiving team starts where the kicking team touched it.

5.3 Kickoff Spot Division Rule

The Junior Division kicks off from its own 10-yard line.

5.4 Downs

The offense has 4 downs to cross midfield. (If the offense fails to cross midfield in 3 downs, it may choose to go for it or punt. If it does not cross midfield after the 4th down, the opposing defense gets the ball where the 4th-down play ended. If the offense chooses to punt, a change of possession occurs and the ball is spotted for the opposing offense at their own 5-yard line.) Once the offense crosses midfield, it has 3 downs to score. If it fails to score in 3 downs, a change of possession occurs and the ball is spotted for the opposing offense at their own 5-yard line.

6. Game Length, Timing & Overtime
6.1 Halves

Two 20-minute halves with a running clock. A half ends when there is “no time” left on the game clock. A half will not end on a defensive penalty — in that case the penalty yards are assessed and another play is run. Five-minute halftime.

6.2 Timeouts

Each team has 1 timeout per half during the regular season, and 2 timeouts per half in the post season.

6.3 Clock Stops

The clock stops when a timeout is called, in the last 2 minutes of the 2nd half when the ball carrier is called “out of bounds” or on an incomplete pass, on an injury, on a “Delay of Game” penalty, and on a defensive penalty. Otherwise the clock runs continuously.

6.4 Change of Possession (Last 2 Minutes)

In the last 2 minutes of the 2nd half during a change of possession, the clock is stopped for up to 30 seconds to allow the offensive and defensive teams to take the field, huddle, and call the next play.

6.5 Play Clock

The offense has 30 seconds to snap the ball once the Referee has spotted it. One “Delay of Game” warning per half is allowed to each team.

6.6 Overtime

In overtime, “Regulation Scoring” for touchdown, PAT, safety, and interceptions applies. The visiting team calls the coin toss to determine which team chooses offense or defense first.

1st overtime: Each team gets one play from the defense’s 5-yard line for one point or the defense’s 10-yard line for two points, and may choose to go for 1 or 2. If the second team on offense fails to beat or match the first team, the game is over; if still tied, the teams switch and play another round.

2nd overtime onward: Both teams must attempt a 2-point conversion from the 10-yard line; overtime ends if one team scores and the other does not, otherwise plays repeat from the 10-yard line until there is a winner. Teams alternate offense or defense each round. There are no timeouts. Interceptions are returnable in overtime and worth 2 points, which ends the game. The Final Score includes all points scored in overtime, and the referee determines which end of the field is used.

7. Players & Participation
7.1 Minimum Players & Forfeit

In the 5-on-5 leagues, a team must have no less than 4 players at the start of a game through completion of the game, or a forfeit will occur.

7.2 Mandatory Playing Time

It is the policy of RAMMS that all players MUST play at least one-half of every game in the field, unless specifically authorized under these rules. Attending practices is very important; players who do not attend practices and/or games may receive less playing time. Any coach who wishes to play a player less than one-half of a game must discuss the situation with the Director of Football Operations and receive permission. Violations result in a game forfeiture.

7.3 Playing-Time Series Rule

No substitutions are allowed, except by injury, during each Offensive or Defensive series. The same players who start a series end the series unless there is an injury. No player shall sit out more than 2 series in succession, regardless of length. The first infraction results in a 15-yard penalty and loss of down; the second and subsequent infractions result in 6 points for the opposing team, plus a 15-yard penalty and loss of down.

8. Substitutions
8.1 Call-Ups Between Divisions Division Rule

It is the intent of RAMMS to avoid loss of play caused by a lack of players. A Junior Division team may fill its roster with substitute players from the Sophomore Division for regular season games. The Director of Football Operations must approve all substitute players.

8.2 Roster Limits & Approval

A team is allowed to substitute players to have a game-day roster of one more player than required to field a team. If a roster player appears so that the team now has more than the minimum number of players, the substitute player is replaced by the roster player and is no longer eligible to play in that game. A substitute player may be called up by a particular team only once per season, subject to the Director’s/Commissioner’s approval.

9. Offense
9.1 Center/Quarterback Exchange

The ball must be snapped between the legs, not off to one side, to start play. The Quarterback may not hand the ball to the center between the legs immediately post-snap.

9.2 Motion & Formations

Only 1 player is allowed in motion at a time. Any number of players may be on or off the “Line of Scrimmage,” but all players except the one motion player must be set for 1 second when the ball is snapped. Legal motion is moving parallel to the line of scrimmage when the ball is snapped.

9.3 Running & No-Run Zones

Runs are not allowed in “NO-RUN ZONES,” located 5 yards from First Downs and 5 yards from End Zones, to avoid “short yardage” or “power running” collisions. The Quarterback is not eligible to run the ball past the “Line of Scrimmage.” Only direct handoffs behind the “Line of Scrimmage” are allowed (multiple handoffs are allowed); laterals or pitches are not allowed anywhere. A player who receives a direct handoff may throw the ball from behind the “Line of Scrimmage.” Spinning and lateral moves left and right are allowed, but jump spins are not.

9.4 No Flag Guarding, Blocking, or Straight Arming

Flag guarding is not allowed. Blocking is not allowed: once a player has the ball, all other offensive players must stop running to avoid screen-blocking or obstructing the defense’s path. Straight arming is not allowed. Once a runner has passed the line of scrimmage or the receiver has caught the ball, all other offensive players shall avoid motion that prevents the defense from pulling the ball carrier’s flag. If a runner dives, leaps, or jumps and creates contact with another player, it is treated as a personal foul (a 15-yard penalty and loss of down).

Flag guarding is the intentional or unintentional act by the ball carrier of using their hands, arms, body, or the ball to deny an opponent the opportunity to pull or remove the flag. These acts include swinging of the arm, a straight arm in the vertical position, dropping and swinging the arm that is carrying the ball, dropping the shoulder and arm to block the pulling of the flags, or other like motions.

Flag guarding carries a 5-yard penalty. If a player behind the line of scrimmage is penalized for flag guarding, the 5-yard penalty is assessed from the line of scrimmage and there is no loss of down. If a player advances the ball beyond the line of scrimmage and is penalized for flag guarding, the 5-yard penalty is assessed from the spot of the foul and the down is not replayed.

9.5 Passing

All passes must be forward and received beyond the “Line of Scrimmage.” (Shovel or screen passes must also be beyond the “Line of Scrimmage.”)

9.6 Pass Clock Division Rule

The Quarterback has a 5-second “PASS CLOCK.” If the Quarterback has not thrown the ball within 5 seconds, the play is dead at the spot the Q.B. last possessed the ball. Once the ball has been handed off, the 5-second rule does not apply.

9.7 Receiving

All players are eligible to receive a pass (including the Quarterback if the ball has been handed off). A player must have one foot in bounds when making a reception.

9.8 Fumbles

If the ball is fumbled anywhere on the field, the play is whistled dead, the ball is marked at the spot of the fumble, and the team that fumbled retains possession.

9.9 Coach on Field (Offense) Division Rule

One Coach is allowed on the field of play while their offense is on the field. The Coach may not interfere with the play on the field in any way.

10. Defense
10.1 Rushing the Quarterback

Any number of players can rush the Quarterback. All players who rush the passer must be a minimum of 10 yards from the “Line of Scrimmage” (as marked by the Referee with a field marker) when the ball is snapped. Players not rushing the passer may defend on the “Line of Scrimmage.” Once a rushing player crosses the line of scrimmage, they cannot intentionally block the pass and must go for the player’s flag; hands up in the air results in a 15-yard penalty plus first down. Passes can be blocked if the defensive player has not crossed the line of scrimmage. Rushing players do not have to announce themselves.

10.2 Rushing the Ball Carrier

If the ball is handed off to a running back or wide receiver behind the line of scrimmage, ALL defenders are eligible to rush the ball carrier, regardless of the line of scrimmage.

10.3 Defenses Allowed

Zone, man-to-man, or any combination of defenses are allowed. “Jamming” receivers or intentional contact is not allowed.

10.4 Interceptions

If the ball is intercepted by the defensive team, that team becomes the offensive team and play continues until the player with possession has their flag removed.

10.5 Coach on Field (Defense)

No coach is allowed on the field of play while the defense is on the field in this division.

11. Penalties
11.1 Offensive Penalties

All offensive penalties are 5 yards unless otherwise noted (* denotes a spot foul, spotted 5 yards from the spot of the foul): illegal motion (more than one person moving), false start, illegal forward pass, offensive pass interference, flag guarding, delay of game, and straight arming*. A Personal Foul (taunting, tackling, tripping, kicking, contact to the head, elbowing, blocking, “jamming” receivers, etc.) is a 15-yard penalty and loss of down; two in a game from the same player results in ejection. Unsportsmanlike Conduct is a 15-yard penalty and loss of down. The ball must be snapped between the legs of the Center.

On flag guarding behind the line of scrimmage, the 5-yard penalty is assessed from the line of scrimmage with no loss of down; if the ball was advanced beyond the line of scrimmage, the 5-yard penalty is assessed from the spot of the foul and the down is not replayed. Penalties are enforced on PAT (extra point) attempts: if a team attempting a 1-point PAT from the 5-yard line is penalized, the ball is spotted at the 10-yard line, and if successfully converted from the 10-yard line the PAT is still awarded only 1 point. Penalties within the 5-yard line are half the distance to the goal line. Penalties may be declined.

11.2 Defensive Penalties

Offside/Encroachment – 5 yards. Pass Interference – 5 yards plus first down. Illegal flag pull (before the receiver has the ball) – 5 yards plus first down. Illegal rushing (starting the rush from inside the marker) – 5 yards. Making contact with the Quarterback’s arm while they are throwing a forward pass – 15 yards plus first down (defenders within arm’s reach should be trying to pull the flag). Personal Foul – 15 yards plus first down. Unsportsmanlike Conduct – 15 yards plus first down.

12. Playoffs
12.1 Format

Playoffs are conducted pursuant to RAMMS Board Policy and are single elimination.

12.2 Reporting & Rules on Hand

Each coach must keep a copy of the rules at every game. The Home Team reports the final score to the League Commissioner within twenty-four (24) hours — preferably immediately — via email, listing the division, both teams, and the final score.

13. Board Policy & Conduct
13.1 One League

A child shall only play in one league.

13.2 Playing Up or Down

A player may play up or down into the next higher or lower division. To play outside their assigned class group, the player must obtain the approval of the Director of Football. To play up, the player must attend tryouts for both the lower and upper divisions; based on the coaches’ evaluation, a decision is made on the proper division for their safety.

13.3 Ejections

Any player, coach, or spectator ejected by the referee, for whatever reason, must immediately leave the field area and is automatically suspended for the next game, regardless of regular or post season. All ejections are reviewed by the RAMMS D&R Committee for possible Code of Conduct violations and, if warranted, further disciplinary action.

13.4 Coach Presence at Practices & Events

The RAMMS-approved Head or Assistant Coach for each team MUST be at every PRACTICE, GAME, or TEAM EVENT. NO EXCEPTIONS. No practices, games, or team events may be conducted without the presence of either the RAMMS-approved Head or Assistant Coach.
