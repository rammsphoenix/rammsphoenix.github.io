RAMMS Volleyball Rules

Rules

Applies to both leagues
Specific to a league (Junior / Senior)
1. Purpose & Participation
1.1 Purpose

The program provides positive, wholesome recreation for the maximum number of youngsters. Adults involved have the opportunity, through their words and actions, to help young people learn to win—as well as lose—with good sportsmanship, while preparing them for further volleyball opportunities.

1.2 Participation

It is RAMMS policy that all players play in every game, as detailed under Player Rotations (Section 6).

2. Match Format & Scoring
2.1 Rally Scoring

The team that wins the point gets the point, regardless of which team served the ball.

2.2 Match Structure

A match consists of up to three games. The first two games are played to 25 with rally scoring; the winning team must win two games and score at least two more points than the opponent. If each team wins one of the first two games, a third game is played to 15.

2.3 Switching Sides

Teams switch sides after the first game. In the third game, teams switch sides once a team scores 8 points.

2.4 Extra Time

If a match ends before the full hour is up, teams may play for fun until 10 minutes prior to the next match.

3. Serving
3.1 Serve Order

The home team serves to start game one; the visiting team chooses which side it will defend. The visiting team serves to start game two. If there is a third game, the home team serves to start.

3.2 Continuing & Rotating the Serve

A team continues to serve so long as it wins the point, or until it has earned 5 consecutive points—at which time the other team serves and does not receive a point for the forced turnover of the serve. A single server may have a maximum of 5 consecutive serves before that player must rotate; serve counts carry over across games, so a player who has already served part of that allotment serves only the remainder before rotating out.

3.3 Net Serves

When serving, if the ball hits the net and goes over to the opposing team, it is a good serve.

3.4 Serving Technique

Serves may be overhand or underhand, with the hand open or closed. The ball may be served anywhere behind the serving line.

3.5 Serving Lines & Wristbands Division Rule

There may be multiple forward serving lines for both leagues to begin the season. Coaches designate where players serve from before the match, and each server must wear a wristband matching their serving line and serve from that line for the entire match. Any player without a wristband serves from regulation distance.

Junior League: To start the season, 3 servers serve from the 10′ line, 3 from 15′ behind the net, and 2 from 20′ from the net. Each week, 1 wristband is removed until each team has only 2 servers from 15′ and all others from regulation distance.

Senior League: To start the season, 1 player may serve from 25′ and everyone else from regulation. After week one, all Senior players serve from regulation.

Any overhand server, in any league, must serve from behind the back line.

4. Ball in Play
4.1 Out of Bounds

The ball is out of bounds if no part of it lands inside or on the boundary line. If the ball hits the gym walls, it is out of bounds.

4.2 Ceiling & Backboard

If a team hits the ball and it strikes the ceiling or basketball backboard, it may be hit again before going over the net—so long as it has not already been hit three times. If it strikes the ceiling or backboard and goes over the net, it is a dead ball and a point is awarded to the team that received the ball (not the team that hit it into the ceiling or backboard).

4.3 Double Hitting

A given player may not hit the ball twice in a row before the ball has gone over the net and a player on the opposing team has hit it.

4.4 Over the Net

A team may hit the ball up to three times before it goes over the net to the opposing team.

4.5 Touching the Net

Players are not permitted to touch the net. If a player touches the net, the other team is awarded the point. Referees may use judgment not to call a net foul when the touch was incidental and had no impact on play.

5. Lineups & Rotation
5.1 Player Rotation Lineup

Each coach prepares a rotation lineup including all players present for the start of the game, and the coaches exchange lineups before the game. The lineup may not change from game to game and must be followed. The only exception is injury: an injured player who leaves the game may return only when it is that player’s turn, per the lineup, to rotate back in.

5.2 Out of Rotation

If a team notices the other is out of rotation, its head coach may call a “player rotation timeout.” Both head coaches then approach the referee. If the referee determines a team is out of rotation, that team is warned and placed in proper rotation, and the team that noticed the error is awarded the ball and one point. If a team is found out of rotation twice in one game, that team forfeits the game.

6. Player Rotations & Substitutes
6.1 Rotation of Play

Six players play at a time, in two rows of three. After a change of serve to your team, players rotate. A new player entering the game enters at the server position; the player leaving the court leaves from the front right position. Every time there is a side-out and the team goes from defense to offense, the serving team rotates. Each rotation requires a new player to enter unless there are no substitutes. The rotation order must remain the same throughout a game, and at the start of the second or third game it continues from where the previous game left off. Coaches are encouraged to let each player “start” a game. No substitutions are permitted except as allowed under minimum-players rules.

6.2 Minimum Players & Forfeit

There must be at least 4 players on each team to commence an official game. If a team does not have 4 players within 10 minutes after the scheduled game time, that team forfeits the match. If a match is forfeited, the teams may use the net for that hour to practice or scrimmage.

6.3 Substitute Players

One substitute player may play for a team in a match if the team has fewer than 6 of its own players present, but in all cases the team must have at least 4 of its own players at the match. A substitute must be in the same or a lower grade as the player being played for and a member of another RAMMS volleyball team. A Junior division player may substitute in the Senior division.

7. Timeouts
7.1 Time Limit & Timeouts

There is no game time limit. A timeout may be called by the coach at any time during a game, until the referee blows the whistle to serve. There is a maximum of one timeout per game per team. A “player rotation timeout” does not count as a regular timeout. (A game is the play to reach 25; a set is the play to win 2 out of 3 games.)

8. Equipment, Gym & Net
8.1 Teams & Gym Maintenance

Home and Visitor teams are designated on the season schedule. Both teams are responsible for putting the nets up and down, storing them, and ensuring the gym is clean before leaving. The Home Team keeps score and posts Line Judges on each side of the court. The Score Keeper and Line Judges must be over 18 unless both coaches agree otherwise before the match. Food and drinks are not allowed in the gym; water bottles are allowed. Each coach must have a copy of the rules at every game. The Home Team provides the referee a game ball and reports the final score to the Commissioner within 48 hours.

8.2 Game Ball Division Rule

Subject to the Commissioner’s discretion, the Senior League uses a Tachikara SV-18S game ball, and the Junior League uses a Tachikara SV-MNC Volley-Lite game ball.

8.3 Net Height

The posts are set so that the top of the net is 7 feet, 4 inches above the floor.

9. Conduct & Officials
9.1 Respect for Officials

The referee, line judges, and score keepers shall be given proper respect. Only the head coach may communicate with the referee and line judges, and only for clarification or interpretation of rules—there is no arguing of judgment calls. The assistant coach may not communicate with the referee or line judges. Parents and others are not allowed to argue calls. A referee or the Commissioners may disqualify and remove a coach, parent, or spectator for behavior contrary to the RAMMS Code of Conduct.

9.2 Coaches Shirts

The head coach and assistant coach of each team will wear the RAMMS-supplied coaches shirt to each game.

9.3 Sidelines

Only RAMMS-approved coaches and team players are allowed on the court or sidelines during games and practices.

10. League Placement & Playoffs
10.1 Board Policy & Playing Up or Down

A child shall only play in one league. A player may play up or down in the next higher or lower league within the Junior (3rd, 4th, and 5th grades) or Senior (6th, 7th, and 8th grades) divisions. To play outside the assigned grade group, the player must attend the evaluation for both leagues and obtain the Commissioner’s approval. Before the league draft, the Commissioner decides whether the player has sufficient talent to play in the upper league safely, or should play in the lower league. The Commissioner may grant an exemption to the evaluation requirement for special circumstances.

10.2 Playoffs

Playoffs will be conducted pursuant to RAMMS Board policy.

11. Safety & Conduct
11.1 Practices

A RAMMS-approved Head or Assistant Coach must be present at every practice, game, or team event—no exceptions. No practices, games, or team events may be conducted without one present.

11.2 Alcohol & Tobacco

All RAMMS events are alcohol- and tobacco-free. No alcohol or tobacco is allowed on the court or sidelines during games and practices.

11.3 Safety Requirements

Proper shoes shall be worn on the court. No watches, necklaces, or personal jewelry shall be worn during play. No casts, whether soft or hard, are permitted under any circumstances. No spectators are allowed on the court during the game.

11.4 Ejections

Any player, coach, or spectator ejected by the referee, commissioner, or assistant commissioner for any reason must immediately leave the area and is automatically suspended for the next game, in the regular season or post season. All ejections are reviewed by the RAMMS D&R Committee for possible Code of Conduct violations and, if warranted, further disciplinary action.
