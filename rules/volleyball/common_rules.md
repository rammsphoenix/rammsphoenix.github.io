# RAMMS Volleyball Rules: Common to Both Leagues

**1. Purpose:** The program provides positive, wholesome recreation for the maximum number of youngsters, helping young people learn to win and lose with good sportsmanship while preparing them for further volleyball opportunities.
**2. Participation:** It is RAMMS policy that all players play in every game, as governed by the rotation rules.
**3. Rally Scoring:** The team that wins the point gets the point, regardless of which team served the ball.
**4. Match:** A match is up to three games. The first two games are to 25 with rally scoring; the winner must win two games and lead by at least two points. If each team wins one of the first two games, a third game is played to 15. Teams switch sides after the first game, and in the third game they switch sides once a team scores 8 points. If a match ends before the full hour, teams may play for fun until 10 minutes before the next match.
**5. Serve Order:** The home team serves to start game one (the visiting team picks which side to defend); the visiting team serves to start game two; the home team serves to start any third game.
**6. Continuing and Rotating the Serve:** A team keeps serving while it wins the point, or until it earns 5 consecutive points, at which time the other team serves and does not receive a point for the forced turnover. A single server may serve a maximum of 5 consecutive times before rotating, and serve counts carry over between games. For example, if a visiting player serving in game one is down 23-21 and hits 4 consecutive aces to win 25-23, the teams switch sides with all players in their spots; since the visiting team serves first in game two, that same server (having served 4 times in game one) gets only one serve before being rotated out.
**7. Net Serves:** When serving, if the ball hits the net and goes over to the opposing team, it is a good serve.
**8. Serving Technique:** Serves may be overhand or underhand, with an open or closed hand, from anywhere behind the serving line. Any overhand server in any league must serve from behind the back line.
**9. Out of Bounds:** The ball is out if no part of it lands inside or on the boundary line, or if it hits the gym walls.
**10. Ceiling and Backboard:** A ball that hits the ceiling or basketball backboard may be hit again before going over the net, provided it has not already been hit three times. If it hits the ceiling or backboard and goes over the net, it is a dead ball and the receiving team is awarded the point.
**11. Double Hitting:** A player may not hit the ball twice in a row before it has gone over the net and an opposing player has hit it.
**12. Over the Net:** A team may hit the ball up to three times before it goes over the net.
**13. Touching the Net:** Players may not touch the net; doing so awards the point to the other team. Referees may use judgment to ignore incidental touches with no impact on play.
**14. Player Rotation Lineup:** Each coach prepares a lineup of all players present at the start, and coaches exchange lineups before the game. The lineup may not change from game to game and must be followed. The only exception is injury: an injured player may return only when it is their turn to rotate back in.
**15. Out of Rotation:** A head coach may call a "player rotation timeout" to flag the other team being out of rotation. If the referee agrees, the offending team is warned and corrected, and the team that noticed is awarded the ball and one point. Being out of rotation twice in one game forfeits the game.
**16. Player Rotations:** Six players play at a time in two rows of three. After a change of serve to your team, players rotate; new players enter at the server position and leave from the front right. The serving team rotates on every side-out from defense to offense. Each rotation requires a new player unless there are no substitutes. Rotation order stays the same within a game and continues across games. No substitutions are permitted except under the minimum-players rule.
**17. Minimum Players:** A team needs at least 4 players to start an official game. A team without 4 players within 10 minutes of the scheduled start forfeits the match; a forfeited match's net may be used for practice or scrimmage that hour.
**18. Substitute Players:** One substitute may play if a team has fewer than 6 of its own players present, but the team must always have at least 4 of its own players. A substitute must be in the same or lower grade as the player replaced and a member of another RAMMS volleyball team; a Junior player may substitute in the Senior division.
**19. Timeouts:** There is no game time limit. A coach may call a timeout any time until the referee whistles to serve, with a maximum of one timeout per game per team. A "player rotation timeout" does not count as a regular timeout.
**20. Teams and Gym Maintenance:** Home and Visitor teams are set on the schedule. Both teams set up, take down, and store the nets and clean the gym. The Home Team keeps score and posts Line Judges on each side; the Score Keeper and Line Judges must be over 18 unless both coaches agree otherwise. Food and drinks are not allowed (water bottles are); each coach must have a copy of the rules. The Home Team provides the referee a game ball and reports the final score to the Commissioner within 48 hours.
**21. Net Height:** The posts are set so the top of the net is 7 feet, 4 inches above the floor.
**22. Officials and Conduct:** Referees, line judges, and score keepers are given proper respect. Only the head coach may communicate with officials, and only for clarification or interpretation of rules; there is no arguing of judgment calls. Assistant coaches, parents, and others may not argue calls. Officials or Commissioners may disqualify and remove anyone for behavior contrary to the Code of Conduct.
**23. Coaches Shirts:** The head and assistant coach of each team wear the RAMMS-supplied coaches shirt to each game.
**24. League Placement:** A child plays in only one league. Players may play up or down between adjacent leagues within Junior (grades 3-5) or Senior (grades 6-8) only by attending both evaluations and obtaining the Commissioner's approval; the Commissioner decides placement before the draft and may grant an evaluation exemption for special circumstances.
**25. Playoffs:** Playoffs are conducted pursuant to RAMMS Board policy.
**26. Practices:** A RAMMS-approved Head or Assistant Coach must be present at every practice, game, or team event, with no exceptions.
**27. Alcohol and Tobacco:** All RAMMS events are alcohol- and tobacco-free; none is allowed on the court or sidelines during games and practices.
**28. Sidelines:** Only RAMMS-approved coaches and team players are allowed on the court or sidelines during games and practices.
**29. Safety:** Proper shoes must be worn. No watches, necklaces, or personal jewelry during play. No casts, soft or hard, are permitted. No spectators on the court during the game.
**30. Ejections:** Anyone ejected for any reason must immediately leave the area and is automatically suspended for the next game, regular or post season. All ejections are reviewed by the RAMMS D&R Committee for possible Code of Conduct violations and further discipline.
