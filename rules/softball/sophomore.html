RAMMS Softball Rules

Sophomore Division

Applies to all divisions
Specific to this division
1. Eligibility & Purpose
1.1 Eligibility Division Rule

Children in third and fourth grade are eligible to play in the Sophomore softball league. A child in another grade may play upon a parent’s request only if approved by the league commissioner. A player wanting to play in an upper league must attend tryouts for BOTH the lower and upper leagues; commissioners may reassign a player who does not, and may grant exemptions for special circumstances.

1.2 Purpose

The intent of the program is to provide clean, wholesome recreation for a maximum number of youngsters, while preparing them for further softball opportunities during their careers.

1.3 Governing Rules

Games are played according to the USSSA Softball Fast Pitch Rules, except as modified herein. The RAMMS Code of Conduct is incorporated herein.

1.4 Participation

All players have the opportunity to play at least three of the first five innings of every game in the field, and must play at least every other inning on defense, disciplinary action notwithstanding.

2. Batting
2.1 Bat-a-Round Order

Batting is on a bat-a-round basis. All rostered players who appear and are not injured are listed in the batting order and given the opportunity to bat in turn.

2.2 Late Arrivals

If a player arrives late, her name is added to the bottom of the batting order.

2.3 Injured or Sick Players

An injured or sick player who can no longer play is not called out when her turn comes up, but no substitute may take her place and the team finishes with its remaining players. She may re-enter, resuming her original spot in the batting order.

2.4 No Bunting

Players are not allowed to bunt.

2.5 Bats

All wood bats are permitted. All non-wood bats must be stamped or otherwise permissible under the current USSSA standards.

2.6 Batter’s Box Division Rule

A player stepping out of the batter’s box will not be called out.

2.7 Six-Run Rule

A team’s time at bat ends after the earlier of three outs or six (6) runs in a half inning. If the team was losing when its at-bat began, it may score enough runs to tie plus an additional six (6). The six-run rule does not limit runs scored while the ball is in play (e.g., a bases-loaded home run counts all four runs).

2.8 Hit By Pitch Division Rule

The primary goal is for the girls to hit the ball, but a batter who is hit and cannot reasonably continue the at-bat should be awarded a base. The batter must attempt to move from the pitch, and there are no coach reliefs during an HBP. A batter hit by a pitch in the air is awarded a base (dead ball); a ball rolling on the ground that touches the batter’s foot does not award a base; a bounced ball that contacts the batter is at the umpire’s discretion.

2.9 Pitching Mask Division Rule

The pitcher is required to wear a pitching mask; masks are highly recommended for any defensive player.

3. Fielding & Pitching
3.1 Defensive Players & Outfield Division Rule

Teams field ten (10) players defensively, with four (4) positioned in the outfield at the same general depth and at least twenty feet (20) behind the infield baseline.

3.2 Base Path & Pitching Distance Division Rule

Base path: 60 ft  ·  Pitching mound: 35 ft

3.3 Player Pitch with Coach Rescue Division Rule

Players pitch with “coach rescue.” The pitcher must start with one foot on and one foot behind the rubber; the same applies to the coach during coach relief, during which the pitcher remains in line with the coach with one foot in the circle. There are no walks: upon the fourth pitch called a “ball” before seven pitches, a coach for the hitting team comes in to throw any remaining pitches (up to seven, unless extended by foul balls or an unhittable pitch). A player may not pitch more than two (2) innings per game; one pitch in an inning counts as a full inning.

3.4 Pitch Count & Strikes Division Rule

A batter receives a maximum of seven (7) pitches or three (3) strikes per at bat. Additional pitches are granted only when the batter fouls the seventh or later pitch. If the umpire deems the seventh pitch unhittable, an additional pitch may be granted.

3.5 Removed Pitcher Division Rule

A player removed as a pitcher may not pitch again in the same game.

3.6 Glove Restrictions

No glove restrictions.

4. Base Running
4.1 No Stealing & Leaving Early Division Rule

Bases may not be stolen. A runner may not leave the base until the pitched ball has been hit by the batter. A runner leaving early is called out after one warning to the player.

4.2 Infield Fly Rule

The infield fly rule is not in effect.

4.3 Crashing Into a Fielder

A runner who deliberately runs into a defensive player to dislodge the ball is called out, the play is dead, and the hitter is awarded first base.

4.4 Obstruction

If a defensive player blocks the base or impedes the runner without possession of the ball (or while not in the act of receiving it), the runner is awarded the base she was proceeding to.

4.5 Blocking Home Plate

The catcher may not block home plate without the ball in her possession.

4.6 Head-First Slides

A runner who makes a head-first slide is called out, except when sliding back to a base she has already reached safely.

4.7 Must Slide to Avoid Contact

Whenever a defensive player is about to receive the ball, or has it and is waiting to make a tag at a base, all runners must slide to avoid physical contact. Violators are called out.

4.8 Stopping Advancement Division Rule

Advancement stops upon an overthrow of first base. A runner past the midpoint hash mark when the infielder gained possession may advance only to the next base, at her own risk and with no risk of error by the defense; a runner not past midpoint may not advance to the next base. Runners may advance on overthrows of any other base.

4.9 Hash Marks

Hash marks are placed midway between first and second, second and third, and third and home, to help the umpire judge whether a runner has passed the halfway mark.

4.10 Pitcher’s Circle Division Rule

The infield has a chalk circle with an 8-foot radius from the center of the front edge of the pitcher’s rubber. When the pitcher controls the ball in the circle, the umpire calls the play dead and runners are placed by their position relative to the hash marks; if the pitcher initiates a play, the ball is live again. A runner who continues to change direction between bases, or stops between bases while the pitcher holds the ball in the circle and is not attempting a throw, may be called out.

4.11 Field Layout

The field is defined by the backstop, the outfield fence, and chalk lines connecting them; where there is no fence, a chalk line, cones, or other marker defines the outfield limit. Any ball crossing this line is a two-base hit and all runners advance two bases. A ball clearly hit over the line in the air is a home run.

5. Game Format
5.1 Innings

A game consists of six (6) innings; no extra innings are played except in the playoffs. An inning starts immediately after the third out of the preceding inning.

5.2 Time Limit Division Rule

A new inning will not start if one hour and thirty minutes has expired. All innings started before 1½ hours are completed; there is no drop-dead time. The game may end in a tie during the regular season.

5.3 Playoffs & Tie Breaker Division Rule

In the playoffs, at least six (6) innings are played, but no new inning starts after 2 hours have expired. If tied after the last completed inning, play continues under the RAMMS California Tie Breaker Rule: the last out of the previous inning starts as a runner at second base with one (1) out, and full innings are played until a winner is determined.

5.4 Game Clock

The umpire maintains the official game clock, and the home team’s scorekeeper records the time of the first pitch in the official scorebook.

5.5 Pace of Play

Any intentional “delays” or “speed-ups” to circumvent the time limit may result in forfeiture at the umpire’s discretion. Curfew: all play must stop no later than 8:45 p.m. (M–Th) and 9:45 p.m. (Fri–Sat). No more than two minutes may elapse between the last out and the first pitch when teams change field positions.

5.6 Minimum Players & Call-ups Division Rule

A team must have at least eight (8) of its own members present at game time or it forfeits. A team with fewer than ten (10) but at least eight (8) of its own players may use a substitute from another RAMMS Sophomore or Freshman Softball team. Subs play the outfield and bat last; the same sub may be used only twice per season and not in consecutive games for one team. Call-ups may only be fourth graders and younger, excluding 3rd- and 4th-grade first- and second-round draft picks, with no more than two (2) called up. All call-ups require commissioner approval; violations are an automatic forfeit.

5.7 Home & Visitor Responsibilities

The home team keeps the official scorebook (recording batting order, runs, outs, and the count in real time), sits in the third-base dugout, and handles trash pickup around it. The visiting team sits in the first-base dugout and handles its own trash pickup.

5.8 Modifications

Modifications and final interpretation of the rules are made by the Sophomore Girls Softball Commissioner. Coaches should check emails regularly for rule updates throughout the season.

6. Field Decorum & Conduct
6.1 Game Sequence & Warm-ups

Players and coaches should report at least thirty (30) minutes before game time. The visiting team may warm up beginning thirty (30) minutes before start, and the home team beginning fifteen (15) minutes before start, each for fifteen (15) minutes of infield/outfield work. No live batting practice is allowed on the field.

6.2 Game Balls & Score Reporting

Each coach must keep a copy of the rules at every game. Each team contributes one (1) new ball and one back-up ball as game balls. The home team enters the final score and reports it to the League Commissioner within forty-eight (48) hours.

6.3 Code of Conduct

The RAMMS Code of Conduct governs all coaches, players, parents, spectators, and officials. As a condition of participation, all must acquaint themselves with and abide by the RAMMS Code of Conduct & Discipline & Rules, available at www.RAMMS.org.

6.4 Time-outs

Time-out is granted by the umpire, not taken by a coach or player, and may be denied when there is no apparent reason or delay is possible. A coach may not leave the dugout area until time-out is granted.

6.5 Coaching Area

Coaches must remain in the area of the dugout except during a time-out — inside the dugout or the space six (6) feet wide immediately in front of it, extending four (4) feet beyond its entrance.

6.6 Appeals

Decisions involving an umpire’s proper judgment may not be appealed. Otherwise, when the ball is dead a coach requests a time-out, makes the appeal directly to the umpire, and the umpire then makes the call.

6.7 Removal & Dugout Eligibility

An umpire may remove a coach or spectators who protest calls or interfere with the game. Only coaches and eligible players may be in the dugout; while at bat, all players except the batter, runners, base coaches, and on-deck batter remain in the dugout.

6.8 Sportsmanship & Cleanliness

Delay-of-game tactics may result in forfeiture. Good sportsmanship is required at all times. Littering is prohibited, and coaches are responsible for cleaning the field and dugout areas before leaving.
