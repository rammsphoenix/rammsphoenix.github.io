RAMMS Softball Rules

Freshman Division

Applies to all divisions
Specific to this division
1. Eligibility & Purpose
1.1 Eligibility Division Rule

Children in second grade and under are eligible to play in the Freshman softball league. A child in another grade may play upon a parent’s request only if approved by the league commissioner.

1.2 Purpose

The intent of the program is to provide clean, wholesome recreation for a maximum number of youngsters, while preparing them for further softball opportunities during their careers.

1.3 Governing Rules

Games are played according to the USSSA Softball Fast Pitch Rules, except as modified herein. The RAMMS Code of Conduct is incorporated herein.

1.4 Participation

All players have the opportunity to play at least three of the first five innings of every game in the field, and must play at least every other inning on defense, disciplinary action notwithstanding.

2. Batting
2.1 Bat-a-Round Order

Batting is on a bat-a-round basis. All rostered players who appear and are not injured are listed in the batting order and given the opportunity to bat in turn.

2.2 Late Arrivals

If a player arrives late, her name is added to the bottom of the batting order.

2.3 Injured or Sick Players

An injured or sick player who can no longer play is not called out when her turn comes up, but no substitute may take her place and the team finishes with its remaining players. She may re-enter, resuming her original spot in the batting order.

2.4 No Bunting

Players are not allowed to bunt.

2.5 Bats

All wood bats are permitted. All non-wood bats must be stamped or otherwise permissible under the most current ASA, USSSA, ISA, ISF or NSA standards.

2.6 Batter’s Box Division Rule

A player stepping out of the batter’s box will not be called out.

2.7 Six-Run Rule

A team’s time at bat ends after the earlier of three outs or six (6) runs in a half inning. If the team was losing when its at-bat began, it may score enough runs to tie plus an additional six (6). The six-run rule does not limit runs scored while the ball is in play (e.g., a bases-loaded home run counts all four runs).

3. Fielding & Pitching
3.1 Defensive Players & Outfield Division Rule

Teams field ten (10) players defensively, with four (4) positioned in the outfield at the same general depth and at least twenty feet (20) behind the infield baseline.

3.2 Base Path & Pitching Distance Division Rule

Base path: 45 ft  ·  Pitching mound: 35 ft

3.3 Coach Pitch Division Rule

Coaches pitch to their own teams for all games. The pitching coach must pitch from the 35-foot distance and remain inside the circle with one foot on the rubber while releasing the pitch, throwing the ball underhand and as flat (parallel to the ground) as possible. The pitching coach will not field the position and will do his/her best to avoid balls hit in his/her direction.

3.4 Pitch Count & Strikes Division Rule

A batter receives a maximum of seven (7) pitches or three strikes per at bat. Additional pitches are granted only when the batter fouls the seventh or later pitch. A batter may not run to first on a dropped third strike. If the umpire deems the seventh pitch unhittable, an additional pitch may be granted.

3.5 Player-Pitcher Positioning Division Rule

Before the pitch, the player-pitcher may not be any closer to home plate than the pitching coach, with one foot on the nine-foot circle line to the first- or third-base side of the pitching coach.

3.6 Batted Ball Striking the Pitching Coach Division Rule

If the umpire rules a batted ball that strikes the pitching coach (buckets, lids, etc. are part of the coach) unavoidable, the play is dead: the batter is awarded first and all runners advance one base. If the contact was avoidable or no effort was made to avoid it, an out is awarded, the play is ruled “no pitch,” and all runners return to their starting base.

3.7 Throw Striking the Pitching Coach Division Rule

Any defensive play on the third-base side of the pitching coach whose throw unavoidably strikes the coach (who remains upright or in the throwing line) results in base runners holding their pre-play positions and the batter being deemed out.

3.8 Glove Restrictions

No glove restrictions.

4. Base Running
4.1 No Stealing & Leaving Early Division Rule

Bases may not be stolen by base runners. A runner may not leave the base until the pitched ball has been hit by the batter.

Enforcement: A runner who leaves the base before the ball has been hit will be called out after one warning to the player.

4.2 Infield Fly Rule

The infield fly rule is not in effect.

4.3 Crashing Into a Fielder

If a base runner deliberately runs into a defensive player in an attempt to cause the defensive player to drop the ball, the runner will be called out, the play called dead, and the hitter awarded first base.

4.4 Obstruction

If a defensive player blocks the base or impedes the runner without possession of the ball (or while not in the act of receiving it), the runner is awarded the base she was proceeding to.

4.5 Blocking Home Plate

The catcher may not block home plate without the ball in her possession.

4.6 Head-First Slides

A runner who makes a head-first slide is called out, except when sliding back to a base she has already reached safely.

4.7 Must Slide to Avoid Contact

Whenever a defensive player is about to receive the ball, or has it and is waiting to make a tag at a base, all runners must slide to avoid physical contact. Violators are called out.

4.8 Stopping Advancement Division Rule

Advancement of the base runners is stopped when an infielder has controlled possession of the ball in hand or glove and is positioned in the infield. Even after advancement is stopped, the base runner may still be put out while attempting to advance or retreat, with no risk of error by the defensive team.

If the umpire deems the base runner to have advanced beyond the midpoint between bases when the infielder gained controlled possession of the ball, the runner may advance only as far as the next base past midpoint, but at her own risk of being put out by the defensive team and with no risk of error by the defensive team. If the umpire finds the runner had not advanced beyond the midpoint when the infielder gained possession, the runner is still at risk of being put out while advancing or retreating (with no risk of error by the defense) and may not advance to the next base past midpoint.

4.9 Hash Marks

Hash marks are placed midway between first and second, second and third, and third and home, to help the umpire judge whether a runner has passed the halfway mark.

4.10 Field Layout

The field is defined by the backstop, the outfield fence, and chalk lines connecting them; where there is no fence, a chalk line, cones, or other marker defines the outfield limit. Any ball crossing this line is a two-base hit and all runners advance two bases. A ball clearly hit over the line in the air is a home run.

5. Game Format
5.1 Innings

A game consists of six (6) innings; no extra innings are played except in the playoffs. An inning starts immediately after the third out of the preceding inning.

5.2 Time Limit Division Rule

A new inning will not start if 1 hour and 30 minutes has expired. All innings started before 1½ hours are completed; there is no drop-dead time. The game may end in a tie during the regular season, but not in the playoffs.

5.3 Playoffs & Tie Breaker Division Rule

In the playoffs, at least six (6) innings are played, but no new inning starts after 1.5 hours have expired. If tied after the last completed inning, play continues under the RAMMS California Tie Breaker Rule: the last out of the previous inning starts as a runner at second base with one (1) out, and full innings are played until a winner is determined.

5.4 Game Clock

The umpire maintains the official game clock, and the home team’s scorekeeper records the time of the first pitch in the official scorebook.

5.5 Pace of Play

Any intentional “delays” or “speed-ups” to circumvent the time limit may result in forfeiture at the umpire’s discretion. Curfew: all play must stop no later than 8:45 p.m. (M–Th) and 9:45 p.m. (Fri–Sat). No more than two minutes may elapse between the last out and the first pitch when teams change field positions.

5.6 Minimum Players & Call-ups Division Rule

A team must have at least eight (8) of its own team members present on the field at the time the game is scheduled to begin, or the team forfeits the game to its opponent if the opponent complies with this requirement. If a team has fewer than ten (10) but at least eight (8) of its own roster players at the start, it may use a substitute player from another team in the RAMMS Freshman Softball leagues, subject to the restrictions below.

Substitute restrictions: (1) A substitute may not play for any one team on that team’s consecutive games, and a team may use the same substitute only twice during the entire season. (2) A substitute must play in the outfield and bat only after all regular players have batted. (3) If a roster player arrives late, that player must be immediately put into the game in place of the substitute.

Call-ups: If a team is not going to have ten (10) of its own players for a game, it may “call up” players from another team as follows: (a) teams may only use second graders and younger from their own division, excluding first- and second-round draft picks; (b) any player who is asked to help out and shows up for a game must be permitted to play; (c) any head coach who uses a player from another team must list both the called-up player’s name and team name (first and last names) to the league Commissioner before the game to get approval. This information is recorded and maintained by the Commissioner or designee, and updated listings of all called-up players are provided to coaches on request.

A team should not call up a player if it expects to have ten (10) of its own. In no case may a team call up more than two (2) players. Any violation of any part of this rule results in an automatic FORFEIT of the game in which the violation occurred. The intent of this rule is to prevent a team from forfeiting when players are missing — it is NOT to build a super team.

5.7 Home & Visitor Responsibilities

The home team keeps the official scorebook (recording batting order, runs, outs, and the count in real time), sits in the third-base dugout, and handles trash pickup around it. The visiting team sits in the first-base dugout and handles its own trash pickup.

5.8 Modifications

Modifications and final interpretation of the rules are made by the Freshman Girls Softball Commissioner or Director. Coaches should read emails to stay current on the most recent rules.

6. Field Decorum & Conduct
6.1 Game Sequence & Warm-ups

Players and coaches should report at least thirty (30) minutes before game time. The visiting team may warm up beginning thirty (30) minutes before start, and the home team beginning fifteen (15) minutes before start, each for fifteen (15) minutes of infield/outfield work. No live batting practice is allowed on the field.

6.2 Game Balls & Score Reporting

Each coach must keep a copy of the rules at every game. Each team contributes one (1) new ball and one back-up ball as game balls. The home team enters the final score and reports it to the League Commissioner within forty-eight (48) hours.

6.3 Code of Conduct

The RAMMS Code of Conduct governs all coaches, players, parents, spectators, and officials. As a condition of participation, all must acquaint themselves with and abide by the RAMMS Code of Conduct & Discipline & Rules, available at www.RAMMS.org.

6.4 Time-outs

Time-out is granted by the umpire, not taken by a coach or player, and may be denied when there is no apparent reason or delay is possible. A coach may not leave the dugout area until time-out is granted.

6.5 Coaching Area

Coaches must remain in the area of the dugout except during a time-out — inside the dugout or the space six (6) feet wide immediately in front of it, extending four (4) feet beyond its entrance.

6.6 Appeals

Decisions involving an umpire’s proper judgment may not be appealed. Otherwise, when the ball is dead a coach requests a time-out, makes the appeal directly to the umpire, and the umpire then makes the call.

6.7 Removal & Dugout Eligibility

An umpire may remove a coach or spectators who protest calls or interfere with the game. Only coaches and eligible players may be in the dugout; while at bat, all players except the batter, runners, base coaches, and on-deck batter remain in the dugout.

6.8 Sportsmanship & Cleanliness

Delay-of-game tactics may result in forfeiture. Good sportsmanship is required at all times. Littering is prohibited, and coaches are responsible for cleaning the field and dugout areas before leaving.
