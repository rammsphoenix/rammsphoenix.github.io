# RAMMS Softball Rules: Common to All Divisions

These rules are shared by the Freshman, Sophomore, and JV divisions.

## Purpose & Eligibility
1. **Purpose:** The program provides clean, wholesome recreation for a maximum number of youngsters while preparing them for further softball opportunities during their careers.
2. **Governing Rules:** Games are played according to the USSSA Softball Fast Pitch Rules, except as modified by RAMMS. The RAMMS Code of Conduct is incorporated.
3. **Participation:** Every player has the opportunity to play at least three of the first five innings in the field, and must play at least every other inning on defense, disciplinary action notwithstanding.

## Batting
4. **Bat-a-Round Order:** Batting is on a bat-a-round basis; all rostered players who appear and are not injured are listed in the order and bat in turn.
5. **Late Arrivals:** A player arriving late is added to the bottom of the batting order.
6. **Injured/Sick Players:** An injured or sick player who can no longer play is not called out when her turn comes up, but no substitute may replace her and the team finishes with its remaining players. She may re-enter in her original batting spot.
7. **No Bunting:** Players are not allowed to bunt.
8. **Bats:** All wood bats are permitted; non-wood bats must be stamped or permissible under applicable standards (ASA, USSSA, ISA, ISF, or NSA).
9. **Six-Run Rule:** A team's at-bat ends after the earlier of three outs or six runs in a half inning. A team that was losing when its at-bat began may score enough to tie plus an additional six. The rule does not limit runs scored while the ball is in play (a bases-loaded home run counts all four runs).

## Fielding
10. **Outfield Depth:** Outfielders play at the same general depth, at least twenty feet behind the infield baseline.
11. **Overthrows Out of Play:** When a thrown ball goes out of the playing area, over the outfield fence, or into the stands or dugout, all runners advance to the base they were running to; a runner past the halfway hash mark advances one additional base (two from the original position).
12. **Glove Restrictions:** None.

## Base Running
13. **Infield Fly Rule:** Not in effect.
14. **Crashing Into a Fielder:** A runner who deliberately runs into a fielder to dislodge the ball is called out, the play is dead, and the hitter is awarded first base.
15. **Obstruction:** A fielder who blocks the base or impedes the runner without possession of the ball (or not in the act of receiving it) gives the runner the base she was proceeding to.
16. **Blocking Home Plate:** The catcher may not block home plate without the ball.
17. **Head-First Slides:** A head-first slide results in an out, except when returning to a base already reached safely.
18. **Must Slide to Avoid Contact:** Runners must slide to avoid contact when a fielder is receiving the ball or waiting to make a tag; violators are out.
19. **Hash Marks:** Placed midway between first and second, second and third, and third and home to help the umpire judge advancement.
20. **Field Layout:** The field is defined by the backstop, outfield fence, and connecting chalk lines (or a marker where there is no fence). A ball crossing the line is a two-base hit; a ball clearly hit over the line in the air is a home run.

## Game Format
21. **Innings:** Six innings; no extra innings except in the playoffs. An inning starts immediately after the third out of the preceding inning.
22. **Regular-Season Time Limit:** A new inning will not start once 1 hour 30 minutes has expired; innings started before 1½ hours are completed. There is no drop-dead time, and regular-season games may end in a tie.
23. **California Tie Breaker:** In playoff ties, the last out of the previous inning starts as a runner at second base with one out, and full innings are played until a winner is determined.
24. **Game Clock:** The umpire keeps the official clock; the home scorekeeper records the time of the first pitch.
25. **Pace of Play:** Intentional delays or speed-ups to beat the time limit may cause forfeiture. Curfew: all play stops by 8:45 p.m. (M–Th) and 9:45 p.m. (Fri–Sat). No more than two minutes between the last out and first pitch on field changes.
26. **Minimum Players & Forfeit:** A team must have at least eight of its own members present at game time or it forfeits. Substitutes play the outfield and bat last, may be used only twice per season, and not in consecutive games for one team. No more than two players may be called up; violations are an automatic forfeit.
27. **Home & Visitor Responsibilities:** The home team keeps the scorebook, sits in the third-base dugout, and handles its trash; the visitor sits in the first-base dugout and handles its trash.
28. **Modifications:** Modifications and final interpretation rest with the division's commissioner (and/or the RAMMS Board). Coaches should monitor emails for updates.

## Field Decorum & Conduct
29. **Warm-ups:** Report at least thirty minutes early; visitors warm up beginning thirty minutes before start, home beginning fifteen minutes before, each for fifteen minutes. No live batting practice on the field.
30. **Game Balls & Reporting:** Each team contributes one new ball and one back-up; the home team reports the final score within forty-eight hours.
31. **Code of Conduct:** The RAMMS Code of Conduct governs all participants and is available at www.RAMMS.org.
32. **Time-outs:** Granted by the umpire, not taken by a coach or player; a coach may not leave the dugout area until granted.
33. **Coaching Area:** Coaches stay in the dugout area except during a time-out — the dugout or six feet in front of it, extending four feet past its entrance.
34. **Appeals:** Umpire judgment calls may not be appealed; otherwise a coach requests a time-out on a dead ball and appeals directly to the umpire.
35. **Removal & Dugout Eligibility:** Umpires may remove coaches or spectators who protest or interfere. Only coaches and eligible players are in the dugout; while batting, all but the batter, runners, base coaches, and on-deck batter stay in the dugout.
36. **Sportsmanship & Cleanliness:** Delay tactics may cause forfeiture; good sportsmanship is required; littering is prohibited and coaches must clean the field and dugout before leaving.
