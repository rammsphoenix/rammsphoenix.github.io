# RAMMS Softball Rules: Division-Specific Rules

The three divisions, youngest to oldest, are **Freshman** (2nd grade & under), **Sophomore** (3rd–4th grade), and **JV / Junior Varsity** (5th–8th grade).

## 1. Eligibility (Grade Levels)
- **Freshman:** Second grade and under.
- **Sophomore:** Third and fourth grade.
- **JV:** Fifth through eighth grade.

(Sophomore and JV add a requirement that a player wanting to move up must attend tryouts for both the lower and upper leagues.)

## 2. Pitching
- **Freshman:** Coach pitch. Coaches pitch to their own teams from the 35-foot distance, one foot on the rubber inside the circle, underhand and as flat as possible. The coach does not field and must do his/her best to avoid balls hit in his/her direction. The player-pitcher sets up no closer than the coach, one foot on the nine-foot circle line to the first- or third-base side of the coach. **Batted ball striking the pitching coach** (buckets/lids are part of the coach): if the umpire rules it unavoidable, the play is dead, the batter is awarded first, and all runners advance one base; if avoidable or no effort was made to avoid it, an out is awarded, the play is ruled "no pitch" (not a ball, not a strike, not counted among the seven pitches), and all runners return to their starting base. **Throw striking the pitching coach** on the third-base side (coach remaining upright or in the throwing line): runners hold their pre-play positions and the batter is deemed out.
- **Sophomore:** Player pitch with "coach rescue." Pitcher must start with one foot on and one foot behind the rubber (same for the coach during coach relief, during which the pitcher remains in line with the coach with one foot in the circle). There are no walks: on the fourth pitch called a "ball" before seven pitches, a coach for the hitting team comes in to throw any remaining pitches up to seven (unless extended by foul balls or an unhittable pitch). A player may pitch a maximum of two innings per game.
- **JV:** Player pitch only — only rostered players from the JV league may pitch in JV regular-season, inter-league, and playoff games. A player may pitch a maximum of three innings per game. Intentional walks are prohibited; at the umpire's discretion, a first offense is a warning and a second offense removes the pitcher from the position and resets the count to the batter.

In all three, one pitch in an inning counts as a full inning for pitcher limits, and a removed pitcher may not pitch again in the same game.

## 3. Pitch Count / Strikes
- **Freshman & Sophomore:** Maximum seven pitches or three strikes per at-bat; extra pitches only on a fouled seventh-or-later pitch; an extra pitch may be granted if the seventh is unhittable.
- **JV:** No pitch limit once the batter has two strikes, regardless of foul balls.

## 4. Base Path & Pitching Distance
- **Freshman:** Base path 45 ft · Pitching mound 35 ft.
- **Sophomore:** Base path 60 ft · Pitching mound 35 ft.
- **JV:** Base path 60 ft · Pitching mound 40 ft.

## 5. Number of Fielders
- **Freshman & Sophomore:** Ten (10) defenders, four (4) in the outfield.
- **JV:** Nine (9) defenders, three (3) in the outfield.

## 6. Leaving the Base / Stealing
- **Freshman & Sophomore:** No stealing by base runners. A runner may not leave the base until the pitched ball has been hit by the batter. **Enforcement:** a runner who leaves early is called out after one warning to the player.
- **JV:** A runner may not leave the base until the pitched ball crosses home plate. **Enforcement:** a runner who leaves before the pitched ball crosses the plate is returned to her base and the runner's team is given a warning; every subsequent violation in the game by any runner from the warned team results in that runner being called out. A runner may advance to any base, including home, after a pitched ball crosses home plate.

## 7. Stopping Advancement / Pitcher's Circle
- **Freshman:** Advancement stops once an infielder controls possession of the ball (in hand or glove) in the infield. A runner may still be put out while advancing or retreating, with no risk of error charged to the defense. A runner past the midpoint hash mark when possession was gained may advance only to the next base past midpoint (at her own risk); a runner not past midpoint may not advance to the next base. Uses a nine-foot pitching-coach circle; there is no player pitcher's-circle look-back rule.
- **Sophomore:** Advancement stops specifically upon an overthrow of first base (runners may advance on overthrows of any other base). The same midpoint-hash-mark and own-risk mechanics apply. Sophomore also uses an 8-foot pitcher's circle (see below).
- **Sophomore & JV — Pitcher's Circle:** An 8-foot-radius chalk circle is measured from the center of the front edge of the pitcher's rubber. (1) When the pitcher controls the ball in the circle, the umpire calls the play dead and runners are placed at the appropriate base per the hash marks (in Sophomore, if the pitcher then initiates a play the ball is live again). (2) Once a runner has rounded a base, if the pitcher holds the ball in the circle and is not attempting or faking a throw, the runner must immediately either advance to the next base or return to the previous base; a runner who keeps changing direction between two bases, or who stops between bases waiting for the pitcher to throw, may be called out by the umpire.

## 8. Batter's Box
- **Freshman & Sophomore:** Stepping out of the box is not an out.
- **JV:** Stepping out is not an out unless the batter steps on home plate during the act of swinging.

## 9. Dropped Third Strike
- **Freshman:** A batter may not run to first on a dropped third strike.
- **Sophomore:** Not addressed (no run on dropped third strike under coach-rescue pitching).
- **JV:** The batter may advance on a dropped third strike (standard conditions: first base unoccupied, or two outs with first occupied; a thrown ball is live).

## 10. Hit By Pitch
- **Sophomore only:** A batter hit in the air is awarded a base (dead ball); a ball rolling on the ground touching the foot does not; a bounced ball is umpire's discretion. The batter must attempt to move; no coach reliefs during an HBP. (Not applicable to coach-pitch Freshman; not specified for JV.)

## 11. Pitching Mask
- **Sophomore only:** The pitcher must wear a pitching mask (recommended for all defenders).

## 12. Metal Cleats
- **JV only:** No metal cleats allowed.

## 13. Playoff Time Cap
- **Freshman:** No new playoff inning after 1.5 hours.
- **Sophomore & JV:** No new playoff inning after 2 hours.

## 14. Minimum Players & Substitute Source
- **Freshman:** Minimum 8 own players; field 10. Subs come from another RAMMS Freshman team. Call-ups: second graders and younger from the same division, excluding 1st/2nd-round draft picks.
- **Sophomore:** Minimum 8 own players; field 10. Subs come from another RAMMS Sophomore or Freshman team. Call-ups: fourth graders and younger, excluding 3rd/4th-grade 1st/2nd-round draft picks.
- **JV:** Minimum 8 own players; field 9. Subs come from another RAMMS Sophomore team. Call-ups: eighth graders and younger from the same division, excluding 1st/2nd-round draft picks.
